# Business Blueprint — Runway Bookkeeping

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run a startup-focused cloud bookkeeping practice as a
business. It is not accounting, tax, or financial advice for any end
client, and must never be treated as a substitute for advice from a
licensed professional. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25
sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Runway Bookkeeping is a fully remote, cloud-native bookkeeping
subscription built specifically for early-stage venture-backed and
bootstrapped startups, delivering investor-ready monthly financials
through heavy automation on modern fintech tools. It's for a
detail-oriented finance professional fluent in startup metrics — a
lower-capital, more automation-heavy alternative to a general
small-business accounting practice.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theBuilder` archetype, with at least
three years of finance experience and genuine fluency in startup
metrics (burn rate, runway, R&D tax credits). This package assumes real
accounting credentials are already in place — it covers the business
side only.

## Business Overview

A fully remote bookkeeping practice delivering automated monthly
financial reporting for startups, built on a cloud fintech stack, with
client acquisition through accelerator and VC network relationships
rather than local professional-services referrals.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from founders who need investor-ready financials without
hiring an in-house finance team. The competitive set includes general
small-business accounting firms (broader focus, less startup-metrics
fluency) and in-house part-time bookkeepers (cheaper but lack
investor-reporting sophistication).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: pre-seed through Series A startups,
venture-backed or bootstrapped, needing monthly investor-ready
reporting. The core need is credible burn-rate and runway visibility
without the cost of a full-time finance hire.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a flat monthly bookkeeping subscription tiered by
expense volume, delivering automated categorization and a monthly
investor-ready reporting package, with fractional-CFO fundraising
support as a premium add-on.

## Revenue Architecture

Flat monthly subscription clients ramp up steadily as new startups sign
on, similar in cadence to a general accounting practice's retainer
growth, but built on predictable flat-tier pricing rather than
transaction-volume billing.

## Pricing Strategy

Flat monthly tiers based on monthly expense volume: €600/month under
€20K monthly expenses, €1,200/month for €20K-75K, €2,200/month for
€75K-200K, plus a €1,500-3,000 annual tax filing add-on.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "investor-ready books, without the in-house finance hire."
Primary channel is startup accelerator and VC network referral
relationships; founder-community content marketing builds secondary
awareness, with fintech/card-issuer partnerships as an experimental
channel.

## Sales System

A free financial-health and runway assessment call is the typical first
conversation, followed by fast software-native onboarding connecting
directly to the startup's bank, card, and Stripe accounts — a roughly
21-day cycle, faster than a traditional accounting-practice sales cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: automated bank/card/Stripe feed
categorization, monthly investor-ready reporting (P&L, burn rate,
runway), quarterly estimated tax and R&D credit review, and annual tax
filing.

## Technology Stack

Built on Ramp for corporate card and spend automation and QuickBooks
Online as the books of record, with Carta as an optional tool for cap
table and investor-reporting context.

## Automation Opportunities

Bank-feed matching, receipt categorization, and expense-policy
enforcement are substantially automated through the fintech software
stack; AI-assisted anomaly detection flags items needing bookkeeper
review.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; the first growth hire is typically a
contractor bookkeeper with genuine startup-metrics fluency, not a
generalist.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €4,000-€10,000 covers software setup,
insurance, and branding — a Low budget tier, leaner than a hybrid
general-practice accounting firm; target monthly income ranges
€7,000-€20,000 with an estimated 7-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against monthly recurring subscription growth, client retention
(understood separately from general SMB churn, since startup client
loss often tracks funding outcomes), gross margin against software and
contractor costs, and churn.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with moderate AI-resistance — the three biggest
risks are the inherently higher churn of a startup client base compared
to general SMBs, dependency on a specific fintech software stack, and
the ongoing burden of regulatory and tax-code changes common to any
accounting practice.

## Competitive Advantages

Heavy fintech automation keeps margin higher than a traditional
practice, investor-ready reporting fluency is a genuine differentiator
general accounting firms don't offer, and fully remote delivery removes
geographic constraints on the client base.

## Launch Strategy

Select and validate the core cloud fintech software stack, obtain
licensing and insurance, then sign the first 2-3 startup clients through
accelerator or founder-network relationships to validate the automated
onboarding flow.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finalize the software stack, licensing, and insurance. Weeks
5-9: onboard and deliver the first monthly investor-reporting package
for 2-3 pilot clients. Weeks 10-13: publicly launch and activate
accelerator/VC referral relationships.

## Scaling Strategy

Sign the first client cohort through accelerator relationships, refine
the automated onboarding and reporting playbook, then hire additional
contractor bookkeepers before introducing a fractional-CFO
fundraising-support tier.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client book and standardized automated workflows make this
sellable to a larger accounting or fintech-services firm.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for transaction categorization and anomaly detection
— implementation accelerators — while keeping investor-reporting
judgment calls and fundraising-support advisory fully professional-led.
