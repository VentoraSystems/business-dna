# Business Blueprint — Lightwell Web Studio

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a templated web design agency as a business. Each `##`
heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2
canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Lightwell Web Studio is a templated web design agency: a solo founder
(plus a subcontracted designer for overflow) builds professional websites
for small and local businesses on proven platforms — Webflow, WordPress,
and Shopify — priced on a published, fixed-price tiered menu rather than
custom quotes. It's for a fast-moving generalist who's comfortable running
sales calls, populating templates, and closing the next project in the
same afternoon. It is not a fit for someone who wants to obsess over one
bespoke, custom-coded build for months — that's `software-house`, a
different business in this same `technology-services` category.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theBuilder` or `theHustler` archetype, with as little as
one year of relevant experience — the templated workflow, not deep
technical mastery, is what makes this achievable quickly.

## Business Overview

A fast-turnaround, template-based web design studio serving small and
local businesses and startups that need a credible web presence without a
six-figure custom-agency budget.

## Market Intelligence

Demand is broad and evergreen — every small business eventually needs a
website — but the market is crowded with freelance marketplaces and,
increasingly, AI website builders competing at the lowest price point. A
published, fixed-price tiered menu and a fast, reliable turnaround are the
differentiators against both.

## Customer Intelligence

Customer type spans both B2B (small business owners) and B2C (solo
professionals). Common pain points are not knowing what a website "should"
cost, and a previous experience with an agency that took months longer
than promised.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-price tiered menu: a landing page, a multi-page
brochure site, or an e-commerce storefront, each with a defined scope and
one round of revisions, followed by an optional monthly care-plan
retainer.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is fast and project-based — short 2-4 week cycles mean cash
arrives frequently in small amounts — supplemented by a slower-building
monthly care-plan retainer base.

## Pricing Strategy

A published, flat per-tier price removes the back-and-forth of a custom
quote and lets most prospects self-qualify before ever booking a call.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "a professional website, live in three weeks, not three
months." Primary channel is local SEO and Google Business visibility;
before/after social content and freelance-marketplace listings build
secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

A low-friction, price-anchored sales motion — most prospects arrive
already knowing the price tier they want, shrinking the sales cycle to
roughly 12 days.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: a discovery questionnaire, template selection
and content population, one structured round of revisions, and launch
into the care-plan retainer.

## Technology Stack

Built on Webflow as the primary platform, with WordPress + Elementor and
Shopify as alternates depending on the client's site type, plus Figma for
design handoff.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted copywriting drafts a first pass of website copy from the
client's discovery questionnaire; AI image generation offers a stock-photo
alternative for clients without their own photography budget.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly at launch — a subcontracted designer is
typically the first addition once project volume exceeds what one person
can deliver within the three-week turnaround promise.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €3,000-€9,000 covers platform licenses, a
portfolio site, and legal setup; target monthly income ranges
€4,000-€12,000 with an estimated 4-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against tiered-menu inquiry-to-close rate, care-plan retention,
care-plan retainer MRR growth, and gross margin per project.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and low AI-resistance — of the three businesses in
this Technology Services batch, this one is most exposed to AI website
builders and marketplace price competition. The two biggest risks are
race-to-the-bottom pricing and platform dependency on Webflow/WordPress.

## Competitive Advantages

A published fixed-price tiered menu, a strict one-round-of-revisions
policy that keeps turnaround under three weeks, and a care-plan retainer
that converts one-time sales into recurring revenue.

## Launch Strategy

Build 2-3 template starting points per tier, publish the pricing menu, and
sell the first few sites — even at a discount — before scaling marketing
spend.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish platform setup, build the template starting points, and
publish the pricing menu. Weeks 5-9: sell and deliver the first 2-3 sites
while enforcing the one-round-of-revisions policy from day one. Weeks
10-13: activate local SEO and social channels, and offer every delivered
client the care-plan retainer at handoff.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by bringing on a second builder or subcontracted designer to run
projects in parallel, then productize the most-requested add-ons
(copywriting, local SEO setup) as fixed-price upsells rather than custom
quotes.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client book, an active template library, and care-plan
retainer contracts are attractive to a larger studio looking to acquire a
proven delivery process and a recurring-revenue client base.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for copy drafting and stock-alternative imagery — the
client relationship and final creative judgment stay human-led, but the
first-draft grunt work is where AI saves the most founder time.
