# Business Blueprint — Willowbrook Veterinary Clinic

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and run a small-animal general veterinary practice as a business
— it is not a substitute for clinical or veterinary-medical training, or
a real regulatory due-diligence process. This is business-planning
content only: no treatment protocols, diagnostic criteria, or clinical
procedures are described anywhere in this document. Each `##` heading
corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2
canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Willowbrook Veterinary Clinic is a small-animal (dog and cat) general
veterinary practice built around a dedicated, unhurried relationship with
one veterinarian, differentiated from rotating corporate veterinary
chains by a recurring wellness-plan subscription that smooths revenue
and keeps preventive care consistent. It's for a licensed veterinarian
with real clinical experience, ready to carry the regulatory, financial,
and staffing responsibility of practice ownership.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theOperator` archetype, requiring at
least four years of clinical experience and full veterinary licensure
before opening. This package assumes licensure and clinical competence
are already in place — it covers the business side only.

## Business Overview

A licensed small-animal veterinary practice delivering wellness exams,
vaccinations, and surgical/dental procedures, supported by veterinary
technicians and front-desk staff, growing through local pet-community
partnerships, local search, and a recurring wellness-plan subscription
that anchors client relationships.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from pet owners seeking consistent, unhurried veterinary
care for their animals. The competitive set includes corporate veterinary
chains (more locations and after-hours coverage, less continuity of
care) and low-cost vaccine clinics (cheaper for single services, no
ongoing relationship or wellness planning).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: pet owners who treat their animals as
family members and want a dedicated relationship with one veterinarian
rather than whichever associate is on shift. The core pain point is
inconsistent care and rushed visits at high-volume corporate chains.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is wellness exams, vaccinations, and surgical/dental
procedures billed per visit, anchored by a monthly wellness-plan
subscription that bundles routine preventive care into a predictable
recurring fee.

## Revenue Architecture

Per-visit fees collect immediately at time of service, while the
wellness-plan subscription creates a predictable recurring revenue base
that smooths the seasonal and discretionary swings typical of per-visit
veterinary billing.

## Pricing Strategy

Per-service fee schedule (e.g. €55 wellness exam, €180-320 spay/neuter,
€250-400 dental cleaning), plus a €35/month wellness-plan subscription
bundling two wellness exams, core vaccinations, and a service discount.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "one vet who actually knows your pet." Primary channel is
local pet-community and shelter partnerships; a strong local Google
review presence builds secondary awareness, with pet-owner social media
content as an experimental channel.

## Sales System

A new-patient wellness exam builds the initial relationship, with the
monthly wellness-plan subscription introduced at that same visit — a
roughly 14-day cycle from first inquiry to booked exam, shorter than a
comparable dental practice since routine pet care is rarely deferred.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: new-patient intake and medical history
collection, in-house diagnostic lab processing and controlled-substance
logging, wellness-plan subscription billing and renewal, and
sterilization/compliance protocols between patients.

## Technology Stack

Built on ezyVet for practice management and scheduling and IDEXX for
in-house diagnostic lab processing, with Weave for client communication.

## Automation Opportunities

Appointment reminders, wellness-plan renewal billing, and vaccination
due-date scheduling are automated; AI-assisted lab result triage flags
priority cases for faster veterinarian review.

## Team Structure

Not solo-friendly — requires veterinary technicians and front-desk staff
from day one; the first growth hire is typically a second veterinary
technician or a part-time associate veterinarian to extend daily
capacity.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €75,000-€170,000 covers exam room and surgical
suite equipment, in-house diagnostic lab equipment, and boarding/kennel
setup; target monthly income ranges €14,000-€42,000 with an estimated
16-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against wellness-plan subscriber retention, gross margin against
equipment and staffing costs, local-partnership and search conversion,
and client attrition.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty with high AI-resistance — hands-on clinical care cannot
be automated. The three biggest risks are very high upfront capital
exposure, malpractice and controlled-substance compliance liability, and
emotionally difficult client conversations around cost and end-of-life
care that require careful staff training beyond clinical skill alone.

## Competitive Advantages

The wellness-plan subscription smooths revenue and keeps preventive care
consistent, recession-resistant demand since pet owners rarely defer
essential veterinary care indefinitely, and a dedicated single-vet
relationship that differentiates against rotating corporate chains.

## Launch Strategy

Secure financing and complete equipment buildout, obtain veterinary
licensure and controlled-substance registration starting 4-6 months
before opening, then open with wellness-plan subscription tiers already
priced and ready to offer at the first visit.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-6: finalize buildout, licensing, and controlled-substance
registration. Weeks 7-10: open for new-patient wellness exams and begin
enrolling clients in the wellness-plan subscription from the first
visit. Weeks 11-13: launch local pet-community partnerships, review
generation, and shelter outreach.

## Scaling Strategy

Fill the founding veterinarian's own schedule and build a stable
wellness-plan subscriber base first, grow surgical and dental procedure
revenue as a share of total revenue, then add a second veterinary
technician or associate veterinarian before considering a second
location.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client panel, wellness-plan subscriber base, and equipment
maintenance records make this sellable to another veterinarian or a
veterinary group — a well-established acquisition path given ongoing
consolidation activity in the category.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for lab-result triage and administrative scheduling
— implementation accelerators — while keeping all clinical care and
patient decisions fully licensed-practitioner-led.
