# Business Blueprint — Pulseline Cycling Studio

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an indoor cycling studio as a business. Each `##`
heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s
v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Pulseline is a high-intensity, music-driven indoor cycling studio
built around 45-minute rhythm-based rides on power-meter-equipped
bikes, with a live performance leaderboard and milestone-ride rewards
driving retention — the second business in the `boutique-fitness-studios`
category, differentiated from the Pilates studio by format (high-intensity
cardio vs. low-impact strength), target customer, and a competitive
leaderboard retention mechanic.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theConnector` or `theHustler` archetype, with at least
two years of relevant instructing experience. Not solo-friendly —
running a full weekly class schedule requires a small team of
certified instructors from day one.

## Business Overview

A studio-based fitness business delivering scheduled, instructor-led
group cycling classes on a fleet of power-meter-equipped bikes, with
real-time performance data displayed on a shared leaderboard.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from cardio-focused fitness enthusiasts seeking a
high-energy group workout experience. The competitive set includes
large gym chains (cheaper, but no curated class experience) and
connected at-home cycling bikes (convenient, but lacking live-room
energy and community).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: individual riders aged roughly 25-40
seeking a high-energy, music-driven cardio workout, distinct from the
injury-recovery and midlife-fitness niche served by the Pilates studio
elsewhere in this category.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a scheduled group ride: riders book a bike online,
attend a 45-minute instructor-led class synced to a curated playlist,
and see their performance (RPM, watts, calories) live on a shared
studio leaderboard.

## Revenue Architecture

Class packs and monthly memberships are paid upfront before classes
are attended, giving a faster cash-collection cycle than a per-visit
business, offset by meaningful upfront bike-fleet and sound-system
capital.

## Pricing Strategy

€30 per drop-in class, a 10-class pack for €255, and a €175/month
unlimited membership — group-format only, with no private one-on-one
sessions offered.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "ride to the beat, leave stronger — a workout that feels
like a night out." Primary channel is local social media content built
around live-class energy clips and instructor personalities; a
discounted intro-ride offer is the secondary conversion channel, with
fitness-app listings and corporate wellness partnerships as an
experimental channel.

## Sales System

A discounted two-ride intro offer lets a prospective rider experience
the live-class energy before committing to a class pack or monthly
unlimited membership — a roughly 10-day cycle, shorter than the
Pilates studio's, reflecting cycling's lower-commitment, more
impulse-friendly first purchase.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: class scheduling and booking, instructor
scheduling and playlist curation, bike fleet maintenance and
telemetry calibration, and membership billing and renewal handling.

## Technology Stack

Built on MINDBODY for class booking and membership management, Stages
Flight for bike performance telemetry and the live leaderboard, and
ClassPass as an optional trial-acquisition channel.

## Automation Opportunities

Class booking, membership billing, and renewal reminders are
automated through studio-management software, but every ride itself
remains fully instructor-led.

## Team Structure

Not solo-friendly — requires a small team of certified instructors
from day one to run a full weekly class schedule.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €15,000-€35,000 covers the bike fleet,
sound and lighting system, and initial software setup — a Medium
budget tier; target monthly income ranges €11,000-€28,000 with an
estimated 13-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against membership renewal rate, gross margin against
instructor wages and lease costs, intro-offer-to-membership
conversion, and churn.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with high AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are meaningful upfront bike-fleet capital, dependency on instructor
charisma and retention, and competition from connected at-home
cycling bikes.

## Competitive Advantages

Recurring membership revenue smooths cash flow, a live leaderboard and
milestone-ride rewards build a competitive social retention loop that
purely appointment-based fitness businesses lack, and the
high-intensity format attracts a cardio-focused segment distinct from
the Pilates studio's low-impact niche.

## Launch Strategy

Secure studio financing and complete the bike-fleet and sound-system
buildout, then run a soft-launch intro-offer period to confirm
class-time demand before finalizing the full weekly schedule.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-6: complete the bike fleet, sound system, and software setup.
Weeks 7-10: run a soft-launch period at reduced capacity, tracking
intro-offer sign-ups by time slot. Weeks 11-13: launch the full public
class schedule with local social content and the intro-ride offer.

## Scaling Strategy

Fill the existing class schedule to consistent capacity first, then
add instructors and additional class times, before considering a
second studio location once the first runs profitably without daily
founder involvement.

## Exit Opportunities

Documented membership records, equipment maintenance history, and
instructor structure make this sellable to a fitness-industry operator
or franchise group.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for attendance-pattern retention alerts — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping every ride itself fully
instructor-led, since the live-room energy cannot be automated.
