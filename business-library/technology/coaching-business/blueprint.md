# Business Blueprint — Clearpath Leadership Coaching

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow a solo executive-coaching practice as a business. Each
`##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s
v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Clearpath Leadership Coaching is a solo executive-coaching practice for
first-time managers and newly-promoted team leads at fast-growing tech
companies. Engagements are sold either directly to the individual manager
or as a company-sponsored L&D contract, always opening with a free
discovery call that clarifies specific leadership gaps. Unlike every other
business in this library, the core deliverable is a live human
conversation — genuinely one of the hardest things for AI tools to
replicate. It's for someone with real, credible management experience who
enjoys developmental conversations more than building products or
systems.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theConnector` archetype, with at least
eight years of hands-on people-management experience. The strongest
`leader` archetype match of any business in this category — this work
rewards genuine lived management credibility more than sales polish.

## Business Overview

A solo executive-coaching practice serving first-time managers and
newly-promoted team leads at fast-growing tech companies, delivered as
package-based 1:1 coaching engagements sold directly or through a
company's L&D budget.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from companies promoting engineers and individual
contributors into management roles faster than they can train them, and
from the new managers themselves who feel unprepared. The competitive set
includes generic online leadership courses (no personalization) and
expensive executive-coaching firms aimed at senior leaders (priced and
positioned above this ICP).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: HR and L&D leaders sponsoring coaching for
their newly-promoted managers, alongside the managers themselves as the
end user. Common pain points are sizing uncertainty for a new manager
about how to handle specific team situations, and an HR sponsor's
skepticism toward vague, unproven coaching claims.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is an individual coaching package (a fixed number of
sessions per quarter), always opening with a free discovery call, with a
cohort-based group coaching program and a corporate L&D retainer contract
as complementary revenue streams.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue builds moderately at first as the HR/L&D sponsor sales cycle plays
out, then becomes predictable through package-based pricing and quarterly
renewal cycles once the first few corporate relationships land — margin is
naturally very high since the core cost is the coach's own time.

## Pricing Strategy

Package-based pricing set after a free discovery call clarifies specific
leadership gaps, sold either directly to the individual manager or
invoiced to the company's L&D budget — this protects against the single
biggest risk in this business: proving soft ROI to a skeptical
budget-holder.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "the management training your promotion didn't come with."
Primary channel is LinkedIn thought-leadership content breaking down real
first-time-manager scenarios; HR/L&D buyer referrals and lunch-and-learn
webinars build secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

A free 20-minute discovery call with the manager or their HR/L&D sponsor
clarifies specific leadership gaps before proposing a coaching package,
with a roughly 30-day cycle given the second stakeholder involved in
company-sponsored engagements.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: an initial assessment and goals-setting
session, biweekly one-on-one coaching sessions, structured progress
check-ins with the manager's own supervisor for company-sponsored
engagements, and an end-of-engagement outcomes review.

## Technology Stack

Built on Calendly for scheduling, Zoom for video sessions, and an
AI-assisted session-notes tool that transcribes and summarizes each
conversation — deliberately the least AI-dependent technology stack of any
business in this library, since the core deliverable is a live human
conversation.

## Automation Opportunities

AI transcribes and summarizes session notes and action items, freeing the
coach to stay fully present during the conversation; AI-assisted analysis
of periodic 360-feedback survey results highlights emerging patterns
between sessions.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly at launch — this is a genuinely personal-
relationship business; an associate coach is typically the first hire
once the coaching methodology is documented well enough to train against.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €2,000-€6,000 — deliberately lean, since the
core asset is the coach's own time, not tooling or inventory; target
monthly income ranges €5,000-€15,000 with an estimated 4-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against discovery-call-to-signed-package close rate, company-
sponsored contract retention across renewal cycles, gross margin
protection against underpricing, and lead conversion from webinars and
referrals into booked discovery calls.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and high AI-resistance — a genuine one-on-one human
coaching relationship is one of the hardest things in this library for AI
to replicate. The three biggest risks are corporate L&D budgets being
among the first cut in a downturn, the founder's own calendar being the
hard ceiling on 1:1 revenue, and the inherently soft, hard-to-quantify
nature of proving coaching ROI to a budget-holder.

## Competitive Advantages

A free discovery call that surfaces concrete leadership gaps before any
commitment, a genuine one-on-one human relationship that AI coaching
tools genuinely cannot replicate, and package-based pricing that gives a
corporate budget-holder a predictable, invoiceable cost.

## Launch Strategy

Complete a coaching certification, set up scheduling and video tooling,
build a simple booking site, then sell the first packages through free
discovery calls with target managers and their HR sponsors.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish certification and tooling setup. Weeks 5-9: run
discovery calls, close the first coaching packages, and deliver the first
full engagement end-to-end. Weeks 10-13: publish the first
thought-leadership content and formalize a referral ask with the first HR
sponsor.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by packaging a cohort-based group coaching program to serve
multiple managers at once without being capped by 1:1 session capacity,
then build a small associate-coach network to take on additional
corporate accounts.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client roster, an associate-coach network, and a proven
coaching methodology are attractive to a larger coaching or L&D firm
looking to acquire a proven delivery process and corporate client base.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for session-notes summarization and survey-pattern
analysis — implementation accelerators — while keeping the actual
coaching conversation and relationship fully human-led.
