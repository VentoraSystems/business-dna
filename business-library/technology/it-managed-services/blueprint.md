# Business Blueprint — Millbrook IT Partners

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow an IT managed services provider (MSP) as a business.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Millbrook IT Partners is an IT managed services provider (MSP): a team of
technicians runs continuous, outsourced IT operations — 24/7 remote
monitoring, patch management, backups, and helpdesk support — for
small-to-mid-size businesses on a tiered per-device or per-user monthly
subscription. Unlike `cybersecurity-consultancy` and
`devops-consultancy`, two other businesses in this same
`technology-services` category, this is not a project or engagement-based
business — the firm operates the client's IT continuously, for as long as
the contract runs. It's for a process-minded operator who finds
satisfaction in a quiet, well-monitored network.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theOperator` or `theBuilder` archetype, with at least four
years of IT-operations experience. This is a steady operations business,
not a project-based sprint — someone chasing novelty every week won't
enjoy the day-to-day rhythm.

## Business Overview

A subscription-based managed IT operations provider serving
small-to-mid-size businesses (20-200 employees) that need an outsourced
IT department rather than a single in-house hire.

## Market Intelligence

Demand is broad and steady — every growing business eventually needs IT
support it can't justify hiring in-house for. The competitive set ranges
from solo "IT guy" freelancers (cheap but no coverage redundancy) to large
national MSPs (comprehensive but impersonal and slower to respond) — a
mid-sized, locally-networked MSP sits deliberately between the two.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: office managers, operations leads, and
small-business owners with 20-200 employees. Common pain points are
unpatched devices discovered only after an incident, and no clear
escalation path when something breaks.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a tiered per-device or per-user monthly subscription
covering monitoring, patching, backups, and helpdesk support, with
separately priced SLA response-time tiers and ad-hoc hardware-refresh
projects available as add-ons.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is subscription-based (per-seat monthly fees) that ramp steadily
as the managed-device count grows client by client — the most
subscription-like revenue shape of any business in this category.

## Pricing Strategy

Transparent, published per-seat pricing tiers remove the "how much will
IT support cost" uncertainty that stalls most MSP sales conversations, and
SLA response-time tiers are priced as a clear, separate upgrade.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "your entire IT department, for less than one in-house
hire." Primary channel is local business-network referrals (chambers of
commerce, accountants, consultants); MSP directory listings and targeted
outbound build secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

A free network and device audit is the standard first conversation,
surfacing existing gaps (unpatched devices, expired backups) before
quoting a per-seat monthly rate, with a roughly 25-day sales cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: 24/7 remote monitoring via RMM tooling,
scheduled patch management and backup verification, tier-1/tier-2
helpdesk ticket triage against SLA targets, and quarterly on-site
hardware review.

## Technology Stack

Built on an RMM (remote monitoring and management) platform like NinjaOne
as the operational backbone, Freshservice for helpdesk ticketing, Veeam
for backup and disaster recovery, and 1Password Business for credential
management.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-driven ticket triage automatically categorizes and routes incoming
tickets by urgency; AI-assisted anomaly detection flags unusual network
or device behavior before it escalates into a client-visible outage.

## Team Structure

Not solo-founder-friendly — a minimum viable team is 2-3 tier-1
technicians to cover business-hours ticket volume plus an on-call
rotation for after-hours critical alerts.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €12,000-€28,000 covers RMM/ticketing platform
setup and initial technician hiring; target monthly income ranges
€10,000-€30,000 with an estimated 8-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against per-seat MRR growth, annual client retention, gross
margin, and free-audit-to-signed-contract close rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and low AI-resistance — of the businesses in this
category, tier-1 helpdesk work is the most exposed to AI-driven
commoditization. The three biggest risks are SLA breaches during staffing
gaps, revenue concentration in large clients, and pricing compression from
AI-driven self-service IT tools.

## Competitive Advantages

Transparent, published per-seat pricing, 24/7 automated monitoring that
catches issues before they become client-visible outages, and a free
initial network audit that makes the sales conversation concrete rather
than hypothetical.

## Launch Strategy

Set up the RMM platform and helpdesk system, hire the first technician,
then sell the first client through a free network audit that surfaces
real, fixable problems.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish platform setup, hiring, and the SLA contract template.
Weeks 5-9: run free network audits and sign the first 2-3 clients, even at
a discounted rate, to prove the operations workflow. Weeks 10-13:
standardize the ticket-triage workflow and publish the tiered pricing
menu publicly.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by hiring additional tier-1 technicians in lockstep with
managed-device count, then introduce a higher-margin, proactive
strategic-IT-advisory tier for clients who've outgrown basic helpdesk
support.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client book, active per-seat SLA contracts, and standardized
monitoring configurations are attractive to a larger MSP looking to
acquire a book of recurring-revenue managed-services clients.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for ticket triage and anomaly detection — the
day-to-day operational grind — while keeping escalation judgment and
client relationship management human-led.
