# Business Blueprint — Meridian Dental Studio

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and run a general/family dentistry practice as a business — it
is not a substitute for clinical training or a real regulatory
due-diligence process. This is business-planning content only: no
treatment protocols, diagnostic criteria, or clinical procedures are
described anywhere in this document. Each `##` heading corresponds to
one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Meridian Dental Studio is a general/family dentistry practice built
around preventive care plus elective cosmetic add-ons (whitening,
veneers) as a margin differentiator beyond pure insurance-driven volume,
offering a modern, unhurried patient experience. It's for a licensed
dentist with real associateship experience, ready to carry the
regulatory, financial, and staffing responsibility of practice ownership
— the highest capital and licensing bar of any business in this library.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theOperator` archetype, requiring at
least five years of clinical experience and full dental licensure before
opening. This package assumes licensure and clinical competence are
already in place — it covers the business side only.

## Business Overview

A licensed dental practice delivering routine preventive and restorative
care plus elective cosmetic services, supported by hygienists, dental
assistants, and front-office staff, growing through insurance panel
placement, local search, and patient referrals.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from patients seeking routine dental care and a modern,
comfortable practice experience. The competitive set includes
high-volume insurance-mill-style practices (cheaper per visit, less
personal) and dental service organization (DSO) chains (more resources,
less individualized care).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: adults 25-55 who want reliable preventive
care and are open to elective cosmetic add-ons once trust is
established. The core pain point is anxiety around clinical, impersonal
dental environments.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is preventive and restorative dental care billed through
insurance, with elective cosmetic services (whitening, veneers)
presented as a follow-up recommendation, plus an in-house membership
plan for uninsured patients.

## Revenue Architecture

Routine care bills and collects relatively quickly, but insurance
reimbursement cycles and larger treatment plans slow overall cash
collection compared to a purely consumer-direct service business.

## Pricing Strategy

Per-procedure fee schedule (e.g. €90 standard cleaning, €700-900 crown,
€350 whitening, €600-900 per veneer), plus an optional €300/year
in-house membership plan covering two cleanings and a service discount
for uninsured patients.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "modern dentistry that doesn't feel clinical." Primary
channel is insurance panel listings and physician/orthodontist referral
relationships; a strong local Google review presence builds secondary
awareness, with cosmetic before/after social content as an experimental
channel.

## Sales System

A new-patient exam, cleaning, and X-rays visit builds the initial
relationship, with elective cosmetic services introduced as a follow-up
recommendation once trust is established — a roughly 30-day cycle from
first inquiry to booked exam.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: new-patient intake and imaging, insurance
verification and claims billing, 6-month recall scheduling, and
sterilization/compliance protocols between patients.

## Technology Stack

Built on Dentrix for practice management and scheduling and DEXIS for
digital imaging, with Weave for patient communication — the most
clinically specialized technology stack of any business in this library.

## Automation Opportunities

Appointment reminders, recall scheduling, and insurance-claim submission
are automated; AI-assisted claims processing flags likely denials before
submission to reduce reimbursement delays.

## Team Structure

Not solo-friendly — requires hygienists, dental assistants, and
front-office staff from day one; the first growth hire is typically a
second hygienist or associate dentist to extend daily capacity.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €75,000-€180,000 covers dental equipment,
imaging, and buildout — the highest budget tier in this library; target
monthly income ranges €15,000-€45,000 with an estimated 18-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against recall-visit return rate, gross margin against equipment
and staffing costs, insurance-referral and local-search conversion, and
patient attrition.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty with high AI-resistance — hands-on clinical care cannot
be automated. The three biggest risks are very high upfront capital
exposure, malpractice liability requiring robust insurance and
documentation, and continuous regulatory/licensing compliance.

## Competitive Advantages

Elective cosmetic services add a real margin differentiator beyond
insurance billing alone, recession-resistant demand since routine dental
care is rarely deferred indefinitely, and a modern patient experience
that differentiates against high-volume insurance-mill practices.

## Launch Strategy

Secure financing and complete equipment buildout, obtain licensure and
insurance panel credentialing starting 4-6 months before opening, then
open with a validated panel of at least 3-4 major insurance networks
already accepted.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-6: finalize buildout, licensing, and insurance credentialing.
Weeks 7-10: open for new-patient exams and begin recall scheduling from
the first visit. Weeks 11-13: launch local SEO, review generation, and
referral outreach to local physicians.

## Scaling Strategy

Fill the founding dentist's own schedule and build a full patient panel
first, grow elective cosmetic revenue as a share of total revenue, then
add a second hygienist or associate dentist before considering a second
location.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented patient panel, staff structure, and equipment maintenance
records make this sellable to another dentist or a dental group —
among the more straightforward exits in this library given established
DSO acquisition activity in the category.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for insurance-claims processing and administrative
scheduling — implementation accelerators — while keeping all clinical
care and patient decisions fully licensed-practitioner-led.
