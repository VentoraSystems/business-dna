# Business Blueprint — Nexus Ledger Co.

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an e-commerce-accounting specialist practice as a
business. It is not accounting, tax, or financial advice for any end
client, and must never be treated as a substitute for advice from a
licensed professional. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25
sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Nexus Ledger Co. is a fully remote bookkeeping and accounting practice
specialized exclusively in multi-channel e-commerce sellers — brands
selling across Shopify, Amazon FBA, Etsy, and wholesale simultaneously.
Where a general small-business accounting practice or a startup-focused
bookkeeping subscription would treat a multi-channel seller as just
another client, Nexus Ledger Co. is built around the three specific
mechanics that make e-commerce books genuinely hard: reconciling sales
and fees across channels, valuing inventory and calculating landed-cost
COGS, and tracking sales-tax-nexus exposure as the client's footprint
grows state by state.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theConnector` archetype, with at
least four years of finance experience and genuine comfort moving
between three distinct technical disciplines at once — channel-fee
reconciliation, inventory/COGS valuation, and sales-tax-nexus rules.
This package assumes real accounting credentials are already in place —
it covers the business side only.

## Business Overview

A fully remote accounting practice built for e-commerce sellers who
have outgrown "I'll just export a CSV from Shopify" bookkeeping — brands
running two, three, or four sales channels at once, with real physical
inventory and real multi-state sales-tax exposure, needing a specialist
rather than a generalist.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from multi-channel e-commerce brands whose books are too
complex for a generalist accounting firm and too retail-specific for a
startup-focused bookkeeping subscription. The competitive set includes
general small-business accounting firms (broad focus, but they treat
channel reconciliation and inventory COGS as an afterthought, not a core
competency) and DIY seller-tool dashboards (cheap, but they don't
reconcile to a clean general ledger or track nexus exposure).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: e-commerce sellers running 2+ sales
channels simultaneously (Shopify, Amazon FBA, Etsy, wholesale), with
real inventory and growing multi-state sales volume. The core need is
an accurate, reconciled general ledger with correct gross margin and a
clear picture of where sales-tax obligations already exist — something
a generalist bookkeeper routinely gets wrong on a multi-channel seller's
books.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a monthly retainer tiered by number of connected
sales channels and monthly order volume, covering multi-channel
reconciliation, landed-cost/COGS accounting, and ongoing nexus-threshold
monitoring — with a standalone nexus exposure review project fee as a
low-friction entry point for sellers unsure which states they already
owe sales tax in.

## Revenue Architecture

Tiered retainer clients ramp up steadily as e-commerce-agency and
Shopify-partner referral relationships compound, similar in cadence to
other accounting-practice retainer growth, but priced meaningfully
higher than a general bookkeeping retainer to reflect the genuinely
harder specialist work involved.

## Pricing Strategy

Tiered monthly retainer based on number of connected sales channels and
monthly order volume, plus a standalone nexus exposure review project
fee (€1,200-€2,500) for sellers unsure which states they already owe
sales tax in, and a sales-tax registration/filing coordination fee for
newly triggered states.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "clean books across every channel you sell on." Primary
channel is referral relationships with e-commerce agencies, Shopify
solution partners, and Amazon-seller-tool vendors; a generalist-
accountant referral network (firms that don't want to handle
multi-channel/inventory complexity themselves) is secondary, with
e-commerce seller-community content marketing on landed cost, FIFO, and
nexus thresholds as an experimental channel.

## Sales System

A free nexus exposure review is the typical low-friction first
engagement — it shows a prospective seller exactly which states they
may already owe sales tax in before they commit to a monthly retainer —
a roughly 25-day cycle that requires walking a technical buyer through
real complexity rather than a flat-tier pricing conversation.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: multi-channel feed integration and
reconciliation (Shopify, Amazon, Etsy, wholesale) matching gross sales,
fees, refunds, and payouts to bank deposits; landed-cost calculation and
FIFO/weighted-average inventory valuation for accurate COGS; economic-
nexus threshold monitoring as sales volume grows; and sales-tax
registration/filing coordination in newly triggered states.

## Technology Stack

Built on A2X for multi-channel sales reconciliation, TaxJar for
sales-tax-nexus monitoring, QuickBooks Online Accountant as the books of
record, with Katana as an optional tool for inventory and landed-cost
management.

## Automation Opportunities

Channel-fee matching, payout reconciliation, and nexus-threshold
tracking are substantially automated through the specialist software
stack once configured; AI-assisted anomaly detection flags fee/refund
irregularities, and AI-assisted forecasting flags when a client's sales
trajectory is approaching a new state's nexus threshold — always
reviewed by a human before any registration or filing action is taken.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; the first growth hire is typically a
specialist bookkeeper fluent in all three core disciplines at once
(multi-channel reconciliation, inventory/COGS, nexus tracking), not a
generalist.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €10,000-€24,000 covers the multi-channel
reconciliation, inventory/landed-cost, and nexus-monitoring software
stack plus insurance and branding — a higher tier than a general
bookkeeping practice because three separate specialist tool categories
need configuring; target monthly income ranges €9,000-€26,000 with an
estimated 8-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against monthly recurring retainer growth, client retention
(understood separately from general SMB churn, since e-commerce client
loss often tracks channel-platform policy shifts or consumer-spending
cycles rather than service quality), gross margin against the
specialist software stack and bookkeeper wages, and churn.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty with moderate AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are the frequent, state-by-state variation in economic-nexus rules
(the sharpest and highest-severity risk this practice carries),
dependency on multiple third-party channel-integration and
nexus-tracking tools, and a client base more cyclical than a general
local SMB book because it's tied to consumer discretionary spending and
channel-platform fee policy.

## Competitive Advantages

Deep, narrow specialization in the three hardest parts of e-commerce
accounting that generalist firms deliberately avoid taking on; a
genuinely low-friction, high-value nexus exposure review as a first
touchpoint; and fully remote delivery with heavy channel-connector
automation that removes both geographic constraints and manual
reconciliation labor.

## Launch Strategy

Select and validate the multi-channel reconciliation, inventory, and
nexus-monitoring software stack, obtain licensing and insurance, then
sign the first 2-3 multi-channel seller clients through e-commerce-
agency or Shopify-partner relationships to validate the onboarding flow.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finalize the software stack, licensing, and insurance. Weeks
5-9: onboard and deliver the first reconciled monthly close, including
landed-cost COGS and nexus status, for 2-3 pilot clients. Weeks 10-13:
publicly launch and activate e-commerce-agency and Shopify-partner
referral relationships.

## Scaling Strategy

Sign the first client cohort through e-commerce-agency and Shopify-
partner relationships, standardize the multi-channel onboarding and
nexus-tracking playbook, then hire additional specialist bookkeepers
before introducing a premium multi-entity/international-nexus advisory
tier for clients expanding into new marketplaces and countries.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client book with standardized reconciliation, landed-cost,
and nexus-tracking workflows makes this sellable to a larger accounting
or e-commerce-services firm.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for multi-channel transaction matching, anomaly
detection, and nexus-threshold forecasting — implementation
accelerators — while keeping landed-cost allocation judgment calls and
final nexus-registration decisions fully professional-led.
