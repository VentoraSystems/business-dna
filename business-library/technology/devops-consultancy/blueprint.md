# Business Blueprint — Foundry Lane DevOps

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow a solo-deliverable DevOps consultancy as a business.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Foundry Lane DevOps is a solo-deliverable DevOps consultancy: a single
senior engineer sells fixed-scope infrastructure and CI/CD pipeline
overhauls to engineering teams at startups and scaleups who already have
a product but a messy, manual, or risky deployment process. Unlike
`it-managed-services`, a different business in this same
`technology-services` category, this is not continuous operations —
it's a bounded engagement that ends with the client's own team able to
run what was built. It's for a senior engineer who's personally been
paged at 3am for a bad deployment before.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theBuilder` or `theOperator` archetype, with at least five
years of production-infrastructure experience. Prior scar tissue from a
real incident is what makes the incremental, rollback-first migration
approach credible to a nervous engineering team.

## Business Overview

A fixed-scope infrastructure and deployment-pipeline consultancy serving
engineering teams that need CI/CD, containerization, or infrastructure-
as-code built or overhauled, but don't have (or don't want to hire) an
in-house DevOps specialist yet.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from engineering teams whose manual, ad-hoc deployment
process has started causing real incidents or slowing releases. The
competitive set includes generalist software houses (weaker on
production-operations judgment) and large managed-cloud-services vendors
(comprehensive but slow and impersonal) — a solo, senior specialist with
a visible incident-response track record sits between the two.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: CTOs and engineering leads at startups and
scaleups. Common pain points are manual, undocumented deployments with no
rollback plan, and unmonitored cloud costs that only get noticed at the
end of the month.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-scope infrastructure project — always preceded
by a technical audit — followed by a mandatory knowledge-transfer phase
and an optional monthly infrastructure-oversight retainer.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is project-fee-based; unlike a maintenance-heavy software build,
a well-built pipeline needs less ongoing hand-holding, so retainer
conversion runs slower than in comparable project-based businesses in
this category.

## Pricing Strategy

Fixed-scope pricing set only after a technical audit quantifies the
client's real infrastructure risk — this makes the sales conversation
concrete and protects margin from the unpredictability of an unaudited
legacy system.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "ship faster and sleep better." Primary channel is
technical case studies documenting real migration timelines and
measurable deployment-speed improvements; CTO-network referrals and
open-source contributions build secondary and experimental credibility.

## Sales System

A technical audit of the client's current pipeline and infrastructure
precedes any fixed-price proposal, with a roughly 35-day cycle from first
conversation to signed project.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: an infrastructure audit and risk assessment, an
incremental migration plan with rollback checkpoints, CI/CD pipeline
build and testing, and mandatory knowledge-transfer sessions before
project close.

## Technology Stack

Built on Terraform for infrastructure-as-code, Kubernetes for container
orchestration, GitHub Actions for CI/CD pipelines, and Datadog for
monitoring and observability.

## Automation Opportunities

AI accelerates writing infrastructure-as-code boilerplate for common
patterns; AI-assisted incident-postmortem drafting is an emerging
practice, always reviewed and refined by the consultant.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly — this is a genuinely one-person-deliverable
business, unlike `mobile-app-development-agency` or
`cybersecurity-consultancy` in this same category, both of which require
a minimum team.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €10,000-€26,000 covers cloud certifications, a
sandbox testing environment, and tooling licenses; target monthly income
ranges €14,000-€38,000 with an estimated 7-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against audit-to-signed-project close rate, gross margin per
project, project-to-retainer conversion (customer retention), and
infrastructure-oversight retainer MRR growth.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty and moderate AI-resistance. The three biggest risks are a
botched migration causing a production outage, the rapid pace of cloud
platform change requiring continuous re-certification, and in-house
engineer resistance to externally imposed changes.

## Competitive Advantages

An incremental migration plan with rollback checkpoints at every stage,
a mandatory knowledge-transfer phase that avoids vendor lock-in, and a
technical audit that makes the sales conversation concrete rather than
abstract.

## Launch Strategy

Get cloud certifications, build the sandbox testing environment, then
sell the first engagement through a technical audit that quantifies a
real prospect's infrastructure risk.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish certifications, the sandbox environment, and the audit
checklist. Weeks 5-9: sell and deliver the first infrastructure audit and
migration, testing every rollback checkpoint before touching production.
Weeks 10-13: run the mandatory knowledge-transfer session, publish the
first case study, and ask for a warm introduction to another engineering
leader.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by bringing on a second senior DevOps engineer for concurrent
projects, then productize a recurring infrastructure health-check
retainer that generates renewed project work as client infrastructure
evolves.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client book, migration playbooks, and any active
infrastructure-oversight retainers are attractive to a larger consultancy
looking to acquire a proven, incident-free delivery track record.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for infrastructure-as-code boilerplate and
first-draft postmortems — implementation accelerators — never for the
migration-risk judgment itself, which stays fully human-led given the
production-outage stakes.
