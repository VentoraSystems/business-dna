# Business Blueprint — Steadywork Automation

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an RPA workflow-automation consultancy as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Steadywork Automation is a robotic process automation (RPA)
consultancy that builds and maintains narrow, rules-based software
bots — not AI agents, not custom software — to eliminate specific,
repetitive manual data-entry and data-transfer workflows between
systems that don't natively integrate, priced per workflow with a
monthly maintenance retainer once live.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theOperator` or `theSpecialist` archetype, with at
least three years of relevant technical or operations experience — a
detail-rigorous operator who enjoys mapping messy manual processes into
precise, deterministic logic.

## Business Overview

A consultancy that identifies one high-value manual workflow at a
time, builds a rules-based RPA bot against the client's existing
systems, deploys it with staff training, and maintains it under a
monthly retainer — deliberately narrower in scope than broad
AI-automation strategy work.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from operations and finance leaders at mid-market
companies drowning in manual data entry between systems without native
integrations. The competitive set includes broad AI-automation
consultancies (less narrowly scoped, more expensive) and in-house IT
teams (capacity-constrained, rarely prioritize back-office automation).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: operations, finance, or back-office
leaders at companies with 20-200 employees who can point to a specific,
visibly painful manual workflow — copying data between systems,
reconciling reports by hand — but lack in-house RPA expertise to fix
it.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-fee, single-workflow automation build,
preceded by a paid automation-opportunity audit that is credited toward
the build if the client proceeds, followed by an optional monthly
maintenance retainer.

## Revenue Architecture

Fixed-fee builds are paid on milestones over a 4-6 week engagement, so
cash arrives relatively fast; the smaller recurring maintenance
retainer builds slower but more predictable revenue on top.

## Pricing Strategy

A paid automation-opportunity audit (€1,500-€3,000, credited toward the
first build) funds discovery; the build itself is a fixed fee
(€3,000-€12,000 depending on complexity); ongoing maintenance is
€400-€1,200/month once the bot is live.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "the copy-paste job your team hates, gone in six weeks."
Primary channel is direct outreach to operations leaders around a
specific, visibly painful workflow; UiPath/Power Automate partner
referrals are secondary; before/after case-study content is
experimental.

## Sales System

A discovery call identifies the highest-value workflow to automate
first; the paid audit maps it in detail and produces a fixed-fee
proposal — a roughly 30-day cycle from first call to signed proposal.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: process discovery and audit, bot development
and testing against real workflow data, deployment and staff handover,
and ongoing bot monitoring under the maintenance retainer.

## Technology Stack

Built on UiPath and Microsoft Power Automate as the primary RPA
platforms, with Process Street as an optional tool for documenting
client SOPs alongside each build.

## Automation Opportunities

Bot health monitoring and basic alerting are automated; the underlying
build work — process mapping and bot logic design — stays deliberately
hands-on, since that judgment is the actual product.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch — build capacity is gated by the
founder's own hours until a documented pattern library is mature enough
to onboard a second bot developer.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €4,000-€10,000 covers RPA platform licensing
and a development environment — a Low budget tier; target monthly
income ranges €5,000-€16,000 with an estimated 6-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against audit-to-build close rate, maintenance-retainer
customer retention, gross margin against platform and hosting costs,
and retainer churn.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with low AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are agentic AI increasingly threatening to commoditize narrow RPA,
"bot fragility" when a client's underlying systems change without
notice, and early-stage revenue concentration in a small client base.

## Competitive Advantages

Deterministic, auditable automation earns more trust than opaque
AI-driven automation for finance-sensitive workflows, a growing
reusable-pattern library shortens delivery time on future engagements,
and low overhead keeps the business viable even at a modest client
count.

## Launch Strategy

Complete RPA platform certification and build 2-3 practice bots against
realistic sample workflows, then validate the paid-audit model with a
first real prospective client before broader outreach.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: complete platform certification and build practice bots.
Weeks 5-8: deliver the first paid audit and sign the first fixed-fee
build. Weeks 9-13: complete the first build, deploy it, and convert the
client onto a maintenance retainer.

## Scaling Strategy

Concentrate outreach on the 1-2 workflow types that convert most
reliably, build a documented reusable-pattern library from every
engagement, then hire a second bot developer once pipeline demand
consistently exceeds one person's build capacity.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented pattern library, signed retainer contracts, and
recurring-revenue history make this sellable to a larger automation or
managed-services firm.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for process-discovery assistance — an
implementation accelerator for finding automation candidates faster —
while keeping bot logic design and client handover fully human-led.
