# Business Blueprint — Kestrel Vale Architecture Studio

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow a solo, licensed-architect-led residential design
studio as a business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25
sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Kestrel Vale Architecture Studio is a solo, licensed-architect-led
residential design practice serving homeowners who want a custom home
designed by a licensed professional rather than an unlicensed designer or
a design-build package deal. This is a genuinely different business from
`real-estate-agency` elsewhere in this library — that business brokers
existing property transactions for a commission; this one designs and
oversees construction of new buildings for a design fee. It's for someone
who has already completed architectural licensure and wants to run a
small, design-led practice directly.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theVisionary` archetype, with at least
six years of architectural training and supervised experience —
licensure itself is a hard gate this business cannot operate without. The
strongest `visionary` and `creator` archetype match of any business in
this category.

## Business Overview

A solo residential architecture practice designing custom homes for
individual homeowners, delivered through either a fixed design fee or a
percentage-of-construction-cost agreement covering design through
construction administration.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from homeowners building or substantially renovating a home
who want a licensed architect's design judgment and legal accountability,
not just a drafter or a design-build contractor's in-house designer. The
competitive set includes larger architecture firms (less personal
attention) and unlicensed residential designers (cheaper, but without the
legal credibility or structural rigor licensure provides).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: individual homeowners, typically referred
by a trusted general contractor or real estate agent. Common pain points
are uncertainty about whether a design will actually be buildable within
budget, and difficulty finding an architect willing to take on a single
custom-home project rather than only large commercial work.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-fee or percentage-of-construction-cost design
agreement covering schematic design through construction administration,
opening with an in-person or video site and program consultation.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue builds the slowest of any business in this library — a single
project runs for months from consultation through construction-
administration close-out — but a percentage-of-construction-cost pricing
option scales fee income with project size without requiring more
marketing effort.

## Pricing Strategy

Either a fixed design fee for a clearly scoped project, or 8-12% of total
construction cost for full design-through-construction-administration
service — the latter aligns the studio's incentive directly with
delivering a buildable, well-specified design.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "residential architecture for people who plan to stay
awhile." Primary channel is referrals from trusted general contractors
and real estate agents; a curated portfolio site and local home-show
networking build secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

An in-person or video site and program consultation reviews the
homeowner's lot, budget, and goals before proposing a design agreement,
with a roughly 45-day cycle given the size and consideration required for
this purchase decision.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: an initial site and lot analysis and
programming session, schematic design and design-development drawing
phases, permitting and code-compliance documentation, and construction-
administration site visits during the build.

## Technology Stack

Built on Revit for BIM modeling, SketchUp for conceptual design, and
Enscape for AI-assisted rendering — deliberately the least AI-dependent
technology stack of any business in this set, since design judgment tied
to legal liability resists automation.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted rendering accelerates producing photorealistic client
presentation visuals; AI-assisted preliminary code-compliance checking
flags potential issues before formal permit submission, always verified
by the licensed architect.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly at launch; a licensed associate architect is
typically the first hire once design standards and review processes are
documented well enough to maintain quality across concurrent projects.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €20,000-€60,000 — the largest of any business
in this set, driven by mandatory licensing, professional liability
insurance, and CAD/BIM software; target monthly income ranges
€8,000-€25,000 with an estimated 10-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against consultation-to-signed-agreement close rate, gross and
net margin protection against mandatory insurance and consultant costs,
and customer retention into repeat projects and referrals.

## Risk Analysis

The highest difficulty and highest AI-resistance of any business in this
set — architectural licensure itself is a multi-year gate, and design
judgment tied to legal liability is hard for AI to replicate. The three
biggest risks are genuine legal/insurance liability exposure from design
errors, long project timelines vulnerable to client-side delays, and
custom residential architecture being a discretionary luxury spend that's
cut first when the housing market softens.

## Competitive Advantages

Licensed-architect-led credibility that an unlicensed designer cannot
offer, a trusted referral network that pre-qualifies serious clients
before the first consultation, and a percentage-of-construction-cost
pricing option that aligns incentives with a buildable design.

## Launch Strategy

Complete licensing and insurance setup, configure CAD/BIM software, then
sell the first design agreement through a referral-driven consultation
with a target homeowner or their contractor/agent.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish licensing, insurance, and software setup. Weeks 5-9: run
initial consultations and begin schematic design for the first signed
client. Weeks 10-13: complete the design-development phase and begin
permitting documentation.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by hiring a licensed associate architect to take on a second
concurrent project, then deepen a referral pipeline with a small number
of trusted general contractors and real estate agents rather than
expanding marketing broadly.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented project portfolio, referral network, and an associate-
architect team are attractive to a larger design or design-build firm
looking to acquire a proven practice and client relationships.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for rendering production and preliminary code
checking — implementation accelerators — while keeping all design
judgment and structural decisions fully under the licensed architect's
direct sign-off.
