# Business Blueprint — Concord Pilates Studio

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run a small-group reformer Pilates studio as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Concord Pilates Studio is a small-group reformer Pilates studio built
around membership-based recurring revenue, targeting women aged 28-55
seeking low-impact strength and mobility work — including an underserved
injury-recovery and midlife-fitness segment. It's for a certified
Pilates instructor ready to lead a small teaching team and manage a
membership studio business.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theOperator` archetype, requiring at
least two years of teaching experience and real comprehensive reformer
certification. No government license is required, but credential
quality matters directly to client trust.

## Business Overview

A small-group reformer Pilates studio delivering low-impact strength
classes capped at 8-10 clients per reformer set, led by a
certified instructor team, growing through referrals and an
intro-offer-to-membership funnel.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from clients seeking low-impact strength training as an
alternative to higher-impact boutique fitness. The competitive set
includes HIIT and cycling studios (higher impact, different customer)
and big-box gyms (cheaper, less personalized, no dedicated reformer
equipment).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: women aged 28-55 seeking low-impact
strength and mobility work, including a meaningful injury-recovery and
midlife-fitness segment. The core need is real strength training that
doesn't strain joints the way high-impact formats do.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is small-group reformer classes sold via class packs or
a monthly unlimited membership, with private one-on-one sessions as an
upsell for clients with specific individual needs.

## Revenue Architecture

Class packs and monthly memberships are paid upfront before classes are
attended, giving the studio a faster cash-collection cycle than a
per-visit appointment business.

## Pricing Strategy

€28 per drop-in class, a 10-class pack for €240, €220/month unlimited
membership, and €90 private one-on-one sessions.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "strength that respects your body." Primary channel is
local social media content plus a client referral program; a
discounted intro offer builds secondary conversion, with corporate
wellness partnerships as an experimental channel.

## Sales System

A discounted two-class intro offer lets a prospective client experience
the studio before committing to a class pack or membership — a roughly
14-day cycle from first inquiry to membership decision.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: class scheduling and booking, instructor
scheduling and payroll, reformer equipment maintenance, and membership
billing and renewal.

## Technology Stack

Built on MINDBODY for class booking and membership management, with
ClassPass as an optional distribution and trial-acquisition channel and
Canva for marketing content.

## Automation Opportunities

Class booking, membership billing, and renewal reminders are automated
through studio-management software; AI-assisted attendance-pattern
analysis flags members at risk of cancelling before they do.

## Team Structure

Not solo-friendly — requires a small team of certified instructors from
day one; the first growth hire is typically an additional instructor to
extend the weekly class schedule.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €10,000-€30,000 covers reformer machines,
studio buildout, and booking software — a Medium budget tier; target
monthly income ranges €12,000-€30,000 with an estimated 14-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against membership renewal retention, gross margin against
instructor wages and lease, intro-offer-to-membership conversion, and
membership cancellation rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with high AI-resistance — hands-on instruction and
real-time form correction require a human instructor. The three biggest
risks are significant upfront equipment capital, class-capacity revenue
ceilings, and dependency on lead-instructor retention.

## Competitive Advantages

Recurring membership revenue smooths cash flow, high repeat-visit
frequency builds strong retention, and the low-impact reformer method
attracts an underserved segment that higher-impact boutique fitness
formats don't serve well.

## Launch Strategy

Confirm reformer machine count against the target class schedule,
secure financing and complete the buildout, then validate class-time
demand with a soft-launch intro-offer period before locking in the full
weekly schedule.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-6: finalize buildout, equipment installation, and instructor
hiring. Weeks 7-9: run a soft-opening schedule at reduced class
capacity. Weeks 10-13: launch the full public schedule with local
social content and a referral-program incentive.

## Scaling Strategy

Fill the existing class schedule to consistent capacity first, then add
instructors and additional class times before considering a second
studio location.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented membership base, equipment records, and instructor
structure make this sellable to another operator or a boutique fitness
group.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for membership-retention attendance-pattern alerts
— an implementation accelerator — while keeping every class itself
fully instructor-led.
