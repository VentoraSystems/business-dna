# Business Blueprint — Sentinel Ridge Security

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow a cybersecurity consultancy as a business. Each `##`
heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2
canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Sentinel Ridge Security is a cybersecurity consultancy: a team of
certified penetration testers sells fixed-fee, time-boxed security audits
and penetration tests to mid-market companies facing a compliance
deadline — a SOC 2 audit, an enterprise customer's security
questionnaire, or a funding-round closing condition. Engagements are
project-based, not ongoing operations: the firm assesses, reports, and
moves on, unlike `it-managed-services`, a different business in this same
`technology-services` category that runs a client's IT continuously.
It's for a certified, hands-on tester who can also write a report an
auditor will accept.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theOperator` archetype, with at least
six years of hands-on penetration-testing experience and real
certifications (OSCP, OSCE) — credibility with technical buyers and
compliance reviewers alike is non-negotiable in this business.

## Business Overview

A project-based cybersecurity consultancy serving mid-market companies
that need an independent, certified security assessment to satisfy a
compliance requirement or close an enterprise deal.

## Market Intelligence

Demand is driven by external compliance deadlines — SOC 2 and ISO 27001
audits, enterprise customer security questionnaires, and investor
due-diligence requirements — rather than general security awareness
alone. The competitive set includes large audit firms (slow, expensive,
generalist) and low-cost automated-scan vendors (fast but shallow,
insufficient for a real compliance review).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: CTOs, security leads, and compliance
officers at mid-market companies under an external deadline. Common pain
points are a previous audit report that an auditor rejected as
insufficient, or an enterprise deal stalled pending a security review.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-fee, time-boxed penetration test or compliance
audit scoped to a defined system boundary, with a signed rules-of-
engagement document, followed by an optional remediation-oversight
retainer.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue arrives as discrete engagement sales clustered around clients'
compliance deadlines — think annual recurring revenue from repeat
engagements, not monthly recurring revenue from ongoing operations.

## Pricing Strategy

Fixed-fee pricing per engagement, scoped to a specific system boundary
defined during scoping — this protects the firm from liability creep and
gives the client budget certainty ahead of an audit deadline.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "find the vulnerability before the attacker does." Primary
channel is compliance-driven referrals from lawyers, auditors, and
investors; security-conference speaking and original vulnerability
research build secondary and experimental credibility.

## Sales System

Compliance-driven, deadline-anchored sales — most engagements are
triggered by an external deadline, with a roughly 30-day cycle from first
conversation to signed engagement.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: a scoping call and signed rules of engagement,
a multi-day active test by two or more certified testers, a
severity-ranked written findings report, and an optional
remediation-verification retest.

## Technology Stack

Built on Burp Suite for web-application testing, Nmap for network
scanning, and Metasploit for exploitation validation — deliberately low
automation, since the value is expert human judgment applied against a
specific system.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted reconnaissance tools accelerate the attack-surface-mapping
phase; AI-assisted report drafting is an emerging practice, always
verified line by line by a certified tester before delivery.

## Team Structure

Not solo-founder-friendly — a minimum viable team is two certified
testers, since most engagements require parallel testing within a fixed
window and a single point of failure is a liability risk in itself.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €32,000-€65,000 covers liability insurance,
team certifications, and testing tooling; target monthly income ranges
€20,000-€55,000 with an estimated 9-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against scoping-call close rate, gross margin per engagement,
annual repeat-engagement rate (customer retention), and annual recurring
engagement revenue (ARR, not MRR — this business runs on annual cycles).

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty and high AI-resistance — the firm's actual value is
independent, creative attacker-mindset judgment that automated scanning
can't replicate. The three biggest risks are liability exposure from an
active test, certified-tester talent scarcity, and evolving compliance
framework requirements.

## Competitive Advantages

A report format built to satisfy compliance auditors on first
submission, a defined rules-of-engagement process that protects both
client systems and firm liability, and original vulnerability research
that builds technical authority.

## Launch Strategy

Obtain team certifications and liability insurance, build the
rules-of-engagement contract template, then sell the first engagement
through a compliance-driven referral rather than cold outreach.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish certifications, insurance, and the engagement contract
template. Weeks 5-9: sell and deliver the first penetration test,
requiring the signed rules-of-engagement document without exception.
Weeks 10-13: standardize the findings-report template against a real
auditor's checklist and ask the first client for a compliance-network
referral.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by adding a second certified-tester pod for concurrent engagements,
then productize a lighter-touch, fixed-fee compliance-readiness
assessment as a lower-priced entry offer that upsells into a full
penetration test.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client book, annual repeat-engagement contracts, and a
formalized testing methodology are attractive to a larger security firm
looking to acquire a proven, auditor-trusted delivery process.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for reconnaissance and first-draft report writing —
implementation accelerators — never for the actual exploitation judgment
or the client-facing findings conversation, which stay human-led and
liability-sensitive.
