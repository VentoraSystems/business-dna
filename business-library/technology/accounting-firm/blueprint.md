# Business Blueprint — Meridian Accounting Partners

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow an accounting practice as a business — it is not tax,
accounting, or financial advice for any end client, and should never be used
as a substitute for qualified professional advice given to a specific person
or business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Meridian Accounting Partners is a small-business bookkeeping and tax
practice built around monthly retainers rather than one-off engagements.
The business snapshot: a certified bookkeeper (or small team) manages the
books for 10-25 small businesses on a flat, tiered monthly fee, files
their annual taxes, and processes payroll. It's for a detail-oriented
operator who prefers steady, recurring client relationships over
one-time project sales. It is not a fit for someone chasing rapid,
venture-scale growth — this is a durable, moderate-margin services
business, not a hits-driven one.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theOperator` or `theSpecialist` archetype — someone who
finds satisfaction in a clean, reconciled ledger and a filed return, not
someone who needs constant novelty. Three-plus years of prior accounting
or bookkeeping experience meaningfully shortens the ramp-up.

## Business Overview

A recurring-revenue accounting and bookkeeping practice serving small
businesses and startups that have outgrown a spreadsheet but aren't
ready for an in-house controller.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for outsourced bookkeeping has grown steadily as more small
businesses adopt cloud accounting software; the competitive landscape
is fragmented, with most competitors being solo practitioners or small
regional firms rather than national players.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: small businesses and startups with 2-50
employees. Common pain points are books that are perpetually weeks
behind and surprise tax bills from unmonitored quarterly estimates.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a tiered monthly retainer covering bookkeeping,
reconciliation, and a guaranteed 5-business-day month-end close, with
annual tax filing and payroll processing available as add-ons.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is subscription-based (monthly retainers) supplemented by
seasonal annual tax-filing fees and hourly advisory work — a
moderate-speed revenue ramp rather than a lump-sum sale.

## Pricing Strategy

Tiered flat-fee pricing keyed to transaction volume, not hourly billing
— this removes the client's biggest objection (an unpredictable
invoice) and rewards the practice for efficiency.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "the outsourced finance department for founders who'd
rather build their business than reconcile their books." Primary
channel is referrals from lawyers, business consultants, and existing
clients; content marketing and chamber-of-commerce sponsorship build
secondary awareness.

## Sales System

A consultative, referral-driven sales motion — a free books-health
assessment is typically the first conversation, with a roughly 30-day
sales cycle from introduction to signed retainer.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: monthly close and reconciliation, quarterly
estimated-tax calculations, annual filing, and payroll runs, all
managed through a cloud client portal.

## Technology Stack

Built on QuickBooks Online Accountant or Xero as the core ledger, Dext
for receipt capture, and Gusto for payroll — a moderate automation
level that still requires human review of every close.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted receipt categorization and bank-feed matching meaningfully
cut the manual data-entry time in every month-end close, freeing staff
hours for client-facing advisory work instead.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; a part-time bookkeeper is typically
the first hire once the practice passes roughly 10 retainer clients.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €8,000-€22,000 covers licensing, software
setup, and first-year professional indemnity insurance; target monthly
income ranges €6,000-€18,000 with an estimated 8-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against monthly recurring revenue, client retention, gross
margin, and referral-to-client conversion rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and moderate AI-resistance. The two biggest risks
are regulatory/tax-code change (requiring continuous retraining) and
client concentration (any single client capped under 20% of monthly
recurring revenue).

## Competitive Advantages

Certified QuickBooks/Xero ProAdvisor status, a 5-business-day month-end
close (faster than most local competitors), and transparent flat-tier
pricing.

## Launch Strategy

Get certified, get licensed and insured, then activate the referral
network — announce the practice through the professional contacts most
likely to send warm introductions, not through cold advertising.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish licensing, insurance, and software setup. Weeks 5-8:
sign the first 2-3 retainer clients, even at an introductory rate, to
prove the close-time promise. Weeks 9-13: standardize the month-end
close checklist and start asking every client for one warm referral.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by adding bookkeeping staff to free the founder for
higher-margin advisory work, then introduce a fractional-CFO tier for
growth-stage clients once the base of standardized retainer clients is
stable.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented client book with standardized SOPs is attractive to
larger regional accounting firms looking to acquire a book of
recurring-revenue clients.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for transactional categorization (receipts, bank
feeds) rather than advisory judgment — the trust relationship with
clients stays human-led even as the mechanical work automates.
