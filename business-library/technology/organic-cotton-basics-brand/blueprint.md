# Business Blueprint — Fieldstate Basics Co.

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an organic-cotton basics e-commerce brand as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Fieldstate Basics Co. is a D2C e-commerce apparel brand selling
everyday wardrobe staples — t-shirts, underwear, basics/loungewear —
made from GOTS-certified organic cotton sourced through a direct-trade
farm co-op relationship, with full batch-level supply-chain
traceability. Sold as a scalable, repeatable, inventory-forward online
storefront with consistent core SKUs and regular restocking, plus a
subscription replenishment option and a smaller wholesale channel —
not a limited-run scarcity model.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theVisionary` or `theConnector` archetype, with at
least two years of relevant experience — a founder with a genuine
sustainability brand vision, paired with the relationship-building
skill to lock in the farm co-op and manufacturing partnerships and
grow a wholesale boutique network.

## Business Overview

A D2C e-commerce apparel brand selling everyday GOTS-certified
organic-cotton basics as consistent core SKUs, restocked on a regular
batch cycle, sold primarily direct-to-consumer online with a
subscription replenishment option, plus a smaller wholesale channel —
manufacturing is fully outsourced to a certified partner, with no
in-house physical production.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from eco-conscious consumers who want everyday staples
they can trust, without researching every claim themselves. The
competitive set includes conventional fast-fashion basics brands
(cheaper, no verifiable sourcing) and scarcity-driven upcycled/craft
fashion labels (authentic but not repeatable or restockable).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: eco-conscious consumers who want
reliable, verifiably sustainable everyday basics and are willing to
pay a moderate premium over conventional fast fashion, active on
social platforms and receptive to lifecycle email/SMS marketing.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a consistent, restocked catalog of core basics SKUs,
supported by a "fresh basics every 3 months" replenishment
subscription and a secondary wholesale channel to sustainable-retail
boutiques.

## Revenue Architecture

Core-SKU e-commerce sales generate a steady baseline from launch, and
the replenishment subscription compounds into predictable recurring
revenue over time — moderate revenue speed, driven by sustained
customer acquisition rather than concentrated launch-event bursts.

## Pricing Strategy

Core basics priced €18-€35 per item; a replenishment subscription
bundle at roughly 15% off standalone pricing for a recurring 3-month
box; wholesale to boutique retailers at roughly 50% off retail.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "wardrobe staples you can trace back to the field they
grew in." Primary channel is supply-chain traceability content
marketing; performance social and lifecycle email/SMS marketing is
secondary; sustainable-retail boutique wholesale outreach is
experimental.

## Sales System

An always-available e-commerce storefront sells core SKUs year-round,
with a prompted subscription option at checkout and post-purchase — a
low-friction, roughly 3-day browse-to-buy cycle, plus direct outreach
to boutiques for wholesale accounts.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: farm co-op and manufacturing-partner
relationship management, core-SKU batch production planning and
inventory forecasting, batch-level traceability documentation, and
D2C/subscription/wholesale fulfillment.

## Technology Stack

Built on Shopify for the e-commerce storefront, Recharge for
subscription billing, Klaviyo for email/SMS marketing, and TrusTrace
as an optional supply-chain traceability platform.

## Automation Opportunities

Subscription billing, reorder triggers, checkout, and inventory-level
alerts are automated; sourcing relationships and certification/
traceability verification remain deliberately hands-on.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch — manufacturing is fully outsourced,
so early capacity is gated by the founder's own marketing and
operations hours until a customer-experience/fulfillment coordinator
is added.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €14,000-€30,000 covers the first batch
production run, e-commerce and subscription platform setup, and the
traceability system — a Medium budget tier; target monthly income
ranges €6,000-€18,000 with an estimated 7-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against subscription-driven MRR growth, storefront-to-buyer
conversion, repeat/subscription customer retention, gross margin
against organic-cotton and certified-manufacturing costs, and
wholesale close rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with moderate AI-resistance — the three biggest
risks are farm co-op/manufacturing-partner concentration, pricing
pressure from lower-cost basics competitors, and the need for
genuinely auditable GOTS certification and traceability claims.

## Competitive Advantages

Verifiable batch-level traceability builds trust generic "sustainable"
claims can't match, a repeatable restocked core-SKU catalog creates
predictable scalable revenue unlike scarcity-driven drops, and the
replenishment subscription increases customer lifetime value.

## Launch Strategy

Negotiate the farm co-op and manufacturing-partner agreements and
launch with an initial core-SKU set, then validate fit and pricing
with a small early customer base at standard pricing before scaling
batch orders.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-5: negotiate supplier agreements and launch the storefront
with the initial core-SKU set. Weeks 6-9: validate with an early
customer base and finalize batch-traceability lookup. Weeks 10-13:
launch the full catalog publicly and begin traceability-story content
marketing.

## Scaling Strategy

Increase batch order volume with the manufacturing partner as demand
grows, then make the replenishment subscription the primary retention
engine, then expand the wholesale boutique network as a stabilizing
secondary channel.

## Exit Opportunities

Documented supplier agreements, a subscription and wholesale customer
base, and a working traceability system make this sellable to a
larger sustainable-fashion or lifestyle brand.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for product-description/SEO content generation and
subscription-churn prediction — implementation accelerators — while
keeping sourcing relationships and traceability verification fully
human-led.
