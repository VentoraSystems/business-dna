# Business Blueprint — Bellwood Realty Group

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow a solo, commission-based residential real estate
agency as a business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25
sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Bellwood Realty Group is a solo, commission-based residential real
estate practice representing buyers and sellers in a specific local
neighborhood, working under a sponsoring brokerage. This is a genuinely
different business from `architecture-studio` elsewhere in this library
— that business designs and legally oversees construction of new
buildings for a design fee; this one brokers transactions of existing
property for a commission. It's for someone who thrives on relationship-
building and negotiation and is comfortable with commission-only income.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theConnector` or `theHustler` archetype, with at least
two years of sales or client-facing experience before licensing. The
strongest `seller` archetype match of any business in this category —
this work rewards genuine sales drive more than technical or design
skill.

## Business Overview

A solo residential real estate agent representing buyers and sellers
under a sponsoring brokerage, earning commission on closed transactions,
growing primarily through neighborhood farming and a past-client referral
engine.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from homeowners buying or selling property who want local,
personal representation rather than a rotating call-center voice or a
purely self-service portal experience. The competitive set includes
listing portals and iBuyer platforms (cheaper, but no personal
representation) and discount/flat-fee brokerages (cheaper commission, but
less service).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: individual homebuyers and sellers in a
specific local neighborhood. Common pain points are not knowing the true
local market value of a home, and distrust of generic portal search
results that lack real local context.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is buyer or seller representation through a standard
commission agreement, opening with a free in-home listing consultation or
buyer-needs interview.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is genuinely lumpy — each transaction takes weeks to months to
close and pays out as a single commission at closing, with no base
salary or retainer to smooth the income between closings.

## Pricing Strategy

Standard commission split (typically 2.5-3% per side) paid at closing,
shared with the sponsoring brokerage per the agent's desk agreement —
there is no separate "product" pricing to set, since compensation is
fully transaction-driven.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "your neighborhood, actually known." Primary channel is
consistently farming a specific target neighborhood through local
sponsorships and mailers; past-client and sphere-of-influence referrals
and paid portal leads build secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

A free in-home listing consultation or buyer-needs interview clarifies
the client's timeline, budget, and must-haves before signing a
representation agreement, with a roughly 60-day cycle given the size and
complexity of a home purchase or sale.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: a listing consultation and comparative market
analysis, professional photography and MLS listing creation, showings
and open houses, and offer negotiation and closing coordination.

## Technology Stack

Built on Follow Up Boss for CRM and lead management, DocuSign for
transaction documentation, and AI-assisted comparative market analysis
tools — a more sales-tooling-focused stack than the design-software-
heavy stack of `architecture-studio` elsewhere in this library.

## Automation Opportunities

AI drafts a first-pass MLS listing description that the agent edits for
accuracy and local flavor; AI-assisted comparative market analysis speeds
up pulling comparable sales data for a listing consultation.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly at launch under a sponsoring brokerage; a
licensed buyer's-agent or transaction coordinator is typically the first
hire once transaction volume from referrals is stable.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €5,000-€15,000 covers licensing, MLS access,
CRM setup, and professional photography — a genuinely medium budget tier;
target monthly income ranges €5,000-€18,000 with an estimated 6-month
path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against consultation-to-signed-agreement close rate, lead
conversion from portals and sphere of influence, customer retention into
repeat transactions and referrals, and net margin after brokerage desk
fees.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and the lowest AI-resistance of any business in this
set — automated valuation models and portal-driven search are already
commoditizing basic listing-search functions. The three biggest risks are
commission-only income being entirely transaction-dependent, high
sensitivity to interest-rate cycles and housing-market downturns, and
listing portals and discount brokerages commoditizing the basic
listing-search-showing function.

## Competitive Advantages

A free listing or buyer consultation that makes the value proposition
concrete, deep walked-the-block neighborhood knowledge that generic
portal search cannot replicate, and a growing past-client referral engine
that reduces reliance on expensive portal-lead spend over time.

## Launch Strategy

Complete licensing, join a sponsoring brokerage, set up MLS and CRM
tooling, then sell the first representation agreement by mining the
agent's own existing sphere of influence before spending on portal leads.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish licensing and brokerage onboarding. Weeks 5-9: run
consultations with existing sphere-of-influence contacts and begin
farming a specific target neighborhood. Weeks 10-13: close the first
transaction and request a referral and testimonial from that client.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by building a repeatable neighborhood-farming system and a strong
past-client referral engine, then bring on a licensed buyer's-agent or
transaction coordinator to handle overflow transaction volume.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented past-client database, referral network, and transaction
history are attractive for a potential team merger or brokerage
partnership, rather than a traditional acquisition.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for listing-description drafting and comparative
market analysis — implementation accelerators — while keeping client
relationships and negotiation fully human-led.
