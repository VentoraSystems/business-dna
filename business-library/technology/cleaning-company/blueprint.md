# Business Blueprint — Brightline Cleaning Co.

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow a residential recurring-cleaning company as a business.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Brightline Cleaning Co. is a residential recurring-cleaning company: the
owner cleans homes personally in the first months, then hires and trains
W2 cleaning technicians, keeping the same 2-person team assigned to the
same recurring clients for continuity. It's for a hands-on operator
comfortable doing the physical work at first, then shifting into hiring,
training, and scheduling. Distinct from a cleaning marketplace app — the
whole value proposition is the opposite of a rotating, unvetted cleaner
every visit.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theOperator` or `theBuilder` archetype. No licensing
required to start, unlike most other businesses in this library — the
real barrier is operational discipline, not credentials.

## Business Overview

A recurring house-cleaning company serving specific target neighborhoods,
earning revenue from bi-weekly and weekly cleaning contracts plus one-time
deep cleans, growing through neighborhood door-hangers and referrals
rather than a marketplace app.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from dual-income households who want reliable, recurring
cleaning without the "different stranger every time" experience common to
app-based marketplaces. The competitive set includes cleaning marketplace
apps (cheaper and more convenient to book, but no team continuity) and
large franchise chains (established brand, but less personal).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: dual-income households in specific
higher-income suburban neighborhoods. The core pain point is inconsistent
quality and a lack of trust from rotating, unknown cleaners.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a recurring bi-weekly or weekly cleaning contract,
opening with a free in-home walkthrough quote based on home size and
condition, with add-on services like inside-oven and interior-window
cleaning available.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue starts fast — a signed recurring contract begins paying out
within days — and compounds predictably as each cleaning team's route
fills with recurring clients.

## Pricing Strategy

Flat per-visit rate by home size and frequency (e.g. €65 bi-weekly for a
2-bedroom home, up to €130 for 4+ bedrooms), billed automatically after
each visit, plus named add-ons like inside-oven (€25) and interior
windows (€35).

## Marketing System

Positioning: "the same trusted team, every single visit." Primary channel
is door hangers and referral discounts saturating one target neighborhood
at a time; a well-reviewed Google Business Profile builds secondary
awareness, with local Facebook groups as an experimental channel.

## Sales System

A free in-home walkthrough quote sets the recurring price up front and
converts directly into a scheduled first visit — a short, roughly 5-day
sales cycle, among the fastest in this library.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: the free walkthrough quote, recruiting and
background-checking cleaning technicians, route scheduling to keep the
same team on the same clients, and post-visit quality-control check-ins.

## Technology Stack

Built on Housecall Pro for scheduling and dispatch and QuickBooks for
invoicing, with Checkr for background checks — a lighter, ops-focused
stack than the design- or CRM-heavy stacks of professional-service
businesses elsewhere in this library.

## Automation Opportunities

Scheduling, automated billing after each visit, and appointment reminders
are automated; AI-assisted chat on the booking page can answer common
pricing questions before a lead calls.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly at launch — the owner cleans directly. The
first hire is a second cleaning team once a genuine client waitlist forms.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €1,500-€6,000 covers supplies, bonding
insurance, and scheduling software — the lowest budget tier in this
library; target monthly income ranges €3,000-€9,000 with an estimated
4-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against recurring-client retention on a stable schedule, walkthrough-
to-signed-contract conversion, gross margin against rising labor and
supply costs, and churn from cancellations.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with high AI-resistance — physical, in-home cleaning
cannot be automated or performed remotely. The three biggest risks are
cleaner no-shows or turnover damaging client trust, the trust risk of
in-home access with untrustworthy staff, and low barriers to entry
letting new competitors undercut on price.

## Competitive Advantages

The same-team-every-visit promise directly solves app-based marketplaces'
biggest complaint, fast revenue speed compared to most businesses in this
library, and genuinely AI-resistant, in-person work.

## Launch Strategy

Set up bonding insurance and supplies, book 5-8 free walkthrough quotes in
one target neighborhood to validate pricing, then complete the first
month of recurring visits personally before starting the door-hanger
campaign.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-3: set up insurance, supplies, and scheduling software. Weeks
4-8: book walkthrough quotes and close the first 3-5 recurring clients.
Weeks 9-13: complete the first month of recurring visits and start the
neighborhood door-hanger and referral campaign.

## Scaling Strategy

Fill the owner's own schedule first, then hire and train a second team
once a genuine waitlist forms, repeating the pattern as recurring revenue
supports it — prioritizing route density in existing neighborhoods before
expanding geographically.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented recurring-client contract list, trained team, and standard
operating procedures make this a straightforward business to value and
sell to another local operator, unlike the relationship-dependent exit
path of a solo professional-service business.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for booking-page customer service and administrative
scheduling — implementation accelerators — while keeping the cleaning
work and client relationships fully human-led.
