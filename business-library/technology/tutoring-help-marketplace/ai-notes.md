## Matching Hints

Best matched to someone strong in management and marketing skills, with
systemOriented and analytical personality traits — this is not a
solo-friendly business, and requires operations discipline across two
distinct customer populations at once. Requires a €30,000-€75,000
budget (the largest in this batch), a high risk tolerance given the
genuine cold-start liquidity challenge, 40-55 hours/week availability,
fully remote work, and no travel requirement. Ideal founder archetypes:
theBuilder, theOperator.

## Generation Hints

Keep generated tone operationally grounded, not generic "edtech
startup" language — this is a two-sided marketplace business, so every
generated section should distinguish demand-side (family) dynamics
from supply-side (tutor) dynamics rather than treating it as a
one-sided service.

## Blueprint Hints

Emphasize the cold-start liquidity problem and the "not solo-friendly"
team requirement in Entrepreneur Fit and Launch Strategy, and the
tutor background-check/minor-safety obligation in Risk Analysis and
Operations System — this is the first genuine two-sided marketplace in
the library and should read differently from the one-to-one service
businesses elsewhere.

## Marketing Hints

Always present channel strategy as two distinct motions — demand-side
family acquisition and supply-side tutor recruitment — never collapse
them into a single generic "marketing plan," since under-investing in
either side breaks the marketplace.

## Financial Hints

Model revenue as commission on gross merchandise value (session
bookings), not a flat fee or subscription; net margin should stay
noticeably lower than the library's one-sided service businesses to
reflect genuine two-sided platform and support overhead, and
break-even should reflect the real time two-sided liquidity takes to
build rather than a typical single-founder service timeline.
