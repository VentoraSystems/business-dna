## Executive Summary

Vector Tech Talent is a tech-specialized recruitment agency that places
software engineers, DevOps/SRE, and data professionals for startups and
scale-ups competing in a tight technical talent market. The business
snapshot: a recruiter who can genuinely, technically screen candidates
themselves — not just source and pitch them — builds a niche pipeline in
one stack, earns a fee on each successful placement, and grows toward
retained-search agreements for senior and leadership technical roles.
It's for a relationship-builder who is also comfortable evaluating
engineering work, not someone relying on resume keyword-matching alone.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theConnector` or `theOperator` archetype — someone who
builds trust quickly with both engineering candidates and hiring
managers, and who can run a fast, high-volume technical pipeline without
losing screening rigor. Three-plus years of experience spanning both
recruiting and genuine technical fluency (enough to run a structured
technical-interview rubric and judge a take-home submission)
meaningfully shortens the ramp-up to a sustainable pipeline.

## Business Overview

A tech-specialized recruitment agency positioned against generalist
recruiters — including recruiters who simply relabel themselves "tech
recruiters" without a real screening competency — on genuine technical
vetting ability and a real placement guarantee.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for recruiters who can actually screen engineering talent is
rising faster than demand for generalist recruiters as the technical
talent market stays persistently tight; the competitive landscape
includes large generalist staffing firms (broad sourcing, shallow
technical vetting) and in-house talent teams at better-funded
competitors for the same candidates.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are startups and scale-ups (10-500 employees) hiring
for software engineering, DevOps/SRE, or data roles, often without deep
in-house technical-recruiting capacity of their own. Their core pain
point is a flood of resume-keyword-matched candidates from generalist
recruiters and job boards who don't survive the client's own technical
interview loop.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a curated, technically-screened candidate shortlist
backed by a placement guarantee, sourced through tech-specific channels
and validated through a structured technical-interview rubric and
take-home assessment coordination — not broad, generalist search.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is placement-driven and can move fast once a technical-niche
pipeline is established, built primarily on contingency fees with a
limited placement-guarantee period, plus an optional retained-search
tier for senior and leadership technical roles.

## Pricing Strategy

Contingency fee (a percentage of first-year salary) with a
replace-or-refund guarantee if the hire leaves within a defined number
of months, plus an optional retained-search tier — an upfront fee plus a
completion fee — for senior/leadership technical roles.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "recruiters who can actually read the code." Primary
channel is direct sourcing from GitHub activity and open-source
involvement; developer-community Slack/Discord presence and referrals
build secondary awareness; sponsoring and attending engineering
conferences is an experimental channel for building direct pipeline.

## Sales System

A consultative, competency-led sale — opening with the structured
technical-screening rubric and a sample scorecard to earn a skeptical
CTO's trust, rather than a generic candidate-volume pitch, with a
roughly 21-day sales cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: client intake and technical role scoping,
tech-specific candidate sourcing (GitHub, developer communities, niche
job boards), structured technical screening (rubric-based interview plus
take-home coordination, verifying real skill against resume
keyword-matching), interview coordination with the client's engineering
team, and placement-guarantee-period follow-up.

## Technology Stack

Built on Bullhorn as the core ATS/CRM, GitHub for direct sourcing,
CoderPad for coordinating live-coding and take-home technical
assessments, with Wellfound and LinkedIn Recruiter as secondary sourcing
channels.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted resume and GitHub-activity screening speeds up the
candidate-pipeline-building process; AI-assisted triage of take-home
submissions flags which ones warrant deeper human technical review —
always followed by a human technical-screening call, never replacing it.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch, provided the founder can personally
run the technical-screening rubric; a second technically-fluent
recruiter is typically the first hire once placement volume justifies
expanding capacity, and requires documenting the rubric well enough to
train someone into it.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €12,000-€30,000 covers ATS setup, a
technical-assessment platform, GitHub/LinkedIn Recruiter sourcing
licenses, and initial developer job-board credits; target monthly
income ranges €6,000-€25,000 with an estimated 6-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against annual placement revenue, search-to-placement close
rate, developer-community and GitHub-sourced lead conversion, and
repeat-client rate among engineering hiring managers and CTOs.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and moderate AI-resistance. The three biggest risks
are tech-sector hiring freezes and engineering layoffs during downturns,
guarantee-period replacement obligations (materially costlier for a bad
technical hire), and the founder's own technical-screening competency
being a scaling bottleneck.

## Competitive Advantages

Genuine technical-screening competency that filters out
keyword-matched false positives, tech-specific sourcing channels that
surface passive candidates invisible to generalist sourcing, and a
rigorous rubric plus a real placement guarantee that build trust with
skeptical CTOs.

## Launch Strategy

Pick one technical niche (a stack or role type — e.g. backend
engineering, DevOps/SRE, or data) and build the screening rubric before
launching broadly — leading every pitch with a sample technical
scorecard, not a generic recruiting pitch.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: set up the ATS, GitHub sourcing access, and the
technical-assessment platform, choosing one technical niche. Weeks 5-8:
complete the first 3-5 placements within that niche only, using the
structured screening rubric on every candidate. Weeks 9-13: standardize
the technical-intake workflow and announce the niche specialization
broadly, leading with the screening rubric itself.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by moving repeat clients from one-off contingency placements to
retained-search agreements for senior/leadership technical roles, then
adding a second technically-fluent recruiter once placement volume
justifies it and the screening rubric is documented well enough to
transfer.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented candidate database, client book, and codified
technical-screening rubrics with a strong placement track record are
attractive to larger agencies looking to acquire a technical-recruiting
specialty rather than build one from scratch.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for resume/GitHub-activity screening and take-home
submission triage, never as a replacement for the human technical-screening
call and the final judgment between client and candidate.
