# Business Blueprint — Clearwater Counseling Practice

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and run a solo private-pay psychotherapy practice as a business
— it is not a substitute for clinical training or a real regulatory
due-diligence process. This is business-planning content only: no
clinical treatment modalities, diagnostic criteria, or intervention
techniques are described anywhere in this document, and no claims are
made beyond what a real practice would state in its own marketing. Each
`##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s
v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Clearwater Counseling Practice is a solo private-pay psychotherapy
practice serving working professionals through a hybrid of telehealth
and in-person sessions, differentiated by evening and early-morning
scheduling that fits around a full workday. It's for a licensed
psychologist or therapist ready to run their own practice — the lowest
capital and most solo-founder-friendly of the three licensed
healthcare-adjacent businesses in this library.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` archetype, requiring at least two years
of post-licensure clinical experience before opening a solo practice.
This package assumes licensure and clinical competence are already in
place — it covers the business side only.

## Business Overview

A licensed solo psychotherapy practice delivering individual therapy
sessions to working professionals, operated directly by the founding
therapist with no required staff, growing through professional referral
relationships and online therapist-directory listings.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from working professionals seeking accessible, flexibly
scheduled mental-health support. The competitive set includes large
online therapy platforms (cheaper, less personal, rotating therapist
assignment) and community mental health centers (lower cost, longer
waitlists, less scheduling flexibility).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: working professionals aged 25-45
navigating anxiety, stress, and life transitions who value a consistent,
private relationship with one therapist and need appointment times that
fit around a full-time job. The core pain point is finding a therapist
with real availability outside standard daytime hours.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is individual therapy sessions delivered via telehealth
or in person, with a discounted monthly 4-session package and a limited
sliding-scale allotment for reduced-fee clients.

## Revenue Architecture

Private-pay sessions are billed and collected directly at time of
service, giving this practice the fastest, most direct cash-collection
cycle of the three licensed healthcare-adjacent businesses in this
library.

## Pricing Strategy

Per-session fee (e.g. €90-120 for a 50-minute individual session), with
a discounted monthly 4-session package and a limited sliding-scale
allotment for reduced-fee clients.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "a steady, private space to work through what's weighing on
you." Primary channel is professional referral relationships with local
psychiatrists and physicians; an online therapist-directory listing
builds secondary awareness, with Employee Assistance Program
partnerships as an experimental channel.

## Sales System

A free 15-minute phone consultation lets a prospective client and the
therapist confirm fit before booking a first paid session — a roughly
7-day cycle from first inquiry to booked consultation, the shortest and
most direct sales cycle in this library.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: free intake consultation calls, session
scheduling and secure telehealth delivery, clinical documentation,
out-of-network superbill generation for clients seeking insurance
reimbursement, and continuing-education/licensure-renewal tracking.

## Technology Stack

Built on SimplePractice for practice management, scheduling, and secure
telehealth delivery, with Alma as an optional insurance-credentialing
and client-matching network.

## Automation Opportunities

Scheduling reminders, intake paperwork, and billing are automated
through practice-management software; AI-assisted session-note templates
speed up administrative documentation between sessions, with the
clinician reviewing and finalizing every note.

## Team Structure

Fully solo-founder-friendly — no staff required at this scale; the first
growth hire, once a waitlist forms, is typically a second associate
clinician to extend capacity.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €3,000-€10,000 covers office setup, practice
software, and licensing/insurance — the lowest budget tier of any
licensed healthcare-adjacent business in this library; target monthly
income ranges €6,000-€15,000 with an estimated 6-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against client-retention rate, gross margin against software and
office-space costs, professional-referral and directory-listing
conversion, and client attrition.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with high AI-resistance — the therapeutic
relationship requires a licensed human clinician. The three biggest
risks are professional liability and confidentiality/compliance
obligations, income directly capped by one practitioner's own caseload
and hours, and the need for deliberate weekly-hour limits to sustain a
full caseload without burnout.

## Competitive Advantages

Low overhead and no required staff keep margin high once a stable
caseload is built, recession-resistant and growing demand for accessible
mental-health support, and a hybrid telehealth/in-person model with
evening/early-morning scheduling that differentiates against
rigid-daytime-hours practices.

## Launch Strategy

Confirm licensure and secure professional liability insurance, set up
HIPAA-compliant practice-management and telehealth software, then launch
with free intake consultation calls to validate pricing and niche fit
before accepting the first paying client.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: confirm licensure, insurance, and practice software setup.
Weeks 5-8: run free intake consultation calls and begin the first paid
sessions with a standing recurring weekly slot for each client. Weeks
9-13: launch professional referral outreach and an online
therapist-directory listing.

## Scaling Strategy

Build a full, stable caseload as a solo practitioner first, then
evaluate raising session rates or bringing on a second associate
clinician to extend capacity once a consistent waitlist forms.

## Exit Opportunities

Documented referral relationships and practice systems, plus (in a fully
compliant, confidential manner) an established client base, make this
transferable to another licensed clinician or a small group practice —
though exit activity in this category is smaller-scale and more
relationship-dependent than in the equipment- and staff-heavy dental and
veterinary practices elsewhere in this library.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for administrative session-note templates and
scheduling reminders — implementation accelerators — while keeping every
clinical decision and every session itself fully licensed-clinician-led.
