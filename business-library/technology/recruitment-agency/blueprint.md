# Business Blueprint — Crestpoint Talent Partners

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a recruitment agency as a business — it is not

never be used as a substitute for advice from a licensed professional given

the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Crestpoint Talent Partners is a niche-specialized recruitment agency
built on contingency and retained-search placement fees. The business
snapshot: a recruiter builds a deep candidate pipeline in one specific
role type or industry, earns a fee on each successful placement, and
grows toward retained-search agreements with repeat clients. It's for a
natural relationship-builder who thrives on a fast, deadline-driven sales
cycle, not someone who needs slow, methodical work.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theConnector` or `theHustler` archetype — someone who
enjoys the hunt for the right candidate and builds trust quickly with
both clients and candidates. Two-plus years of prior recruiting
experience meaningfully shortens the ramp-up to a sustainable pipeline.

## Business Overview

A niche-specialized recruitment agency positioned against generalist
recruiters on pipeline depth and a genuine placement guarantee.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for niche-specialized recruiters is rising faster than demand for
generalist agencies as roles become more technical; the competitive
landscape includes large staffing firms (broad but shallow) and other
independents competing on niche depth.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are small-to-mid-sized companies (10-500 employees)
hiring for a specific, hard-to-fill role type. Their core pain point is
getting a flood of unqualified resumes from generalist job-board
postings.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a curated candidate shortlist backed by a placement
guarantee, sourced through deep niche specialization rather than
broad, generalist search.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is placement-driven and can move fast once a niche pipeline is
established, blending contingency fees with growing retained-search
revenue.

## Pricing Strategy

Contingency fee (a percentage of first-year salary) or retained search
with an upfront fee plus a completion fee.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "the right hire, not just a fast one." Primary channel is
referrals from hiring managers and placed candidates; LinkedIn sourcing
and employer-branding content build secondary awareness.

## Sales System

A consultative, candidate-pipeline-led sale — leading with a strong
shortlist example rather than a cold pitch, with a roughly 30-day sales
cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: client intake and role scoping, candidate
sourcing and screening, interview coordination, and placement-guarantee-
period follow-up.

## Technology Stack

Built on Bullhorn as the core ATS/CRM and LinkedIn Recruiter for
sourcing.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted resume screening and sourcing automation speed up the
candidate-pipeline-building process, always followed by a human
screening call.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; a second recruiter is typically the
first hire once placement volume justifies expanding capacity.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €10,000-€30,000 covers ATS setup, LinkedIn
Recruiter licensing, and initial job-board credits; target monthly
income ranges €5,000-€22,000 with an estimated 8-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against annual placement revenue, search-to-placement close
rate, referral-lead conversion, and repeat-client rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and moderate AI-resistance. The two biggest risks
are hiring freezes during economic downturns and guarantee-period
replacement obligations.

## Competitive Advantages

Niche specialization producing a deeper candidate pipeline, a strong
LinkedIn-sourcing presence, and a genuine placement guarantee that
builds client trust.

## Launch Strategy

Pick one niche and build the pipeline before launching broadly — leading
every pitch with a niche-specific candidate example, not a generic
recruiting pitch.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: set up the ATS and LinkedIn Recruiter, choosing one niche.
Weeks 5-8: complete the first 3-5 placements within that niche only.
Weeks 9-13: standardize the intake workflow and announce the niche
specialization broadly.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by moving repeat clients from one-off contingency placements to
retained-search agreements, then adding a second recruiter once
placement volume justifies it.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented candidate database and client book with a strong
placement track record is attractive to larger agencies looking to
acquire a niche specialty.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for resume screening and sourcing, never as a
replacement for the human screening call and negotiation between client
and candidate.
