# Business Blueprint — Keystone P&C Brokerage

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an independent P&C insurance brokerage as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure. Nothing here is
insurance, coverage, or claims advice for any end client._

## Executive Summary

Keystone is an independent property & casualty insurance brokerage
specializing in landlords and rental-property investors with
multi-property portfolios, combining admitted-carrier commission with
surplus-lines broker fees for hard-to-place risks — deliberately
differentiated from the general personal-lines brokerage elsewhere in
this category by niche, sales approach, and revenue model.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theConnector` archetype, with at
least four years of relevant experience. Solo-friendly at launch,
matching the general brokerage's precedent.

## Business Overview

An independent insurance brokerage placing landlord policies, umbrella
liability, builder's-risk coverage, and hard-to-place surplus-lines
risks across multiple carriers for clients who own more than one
rental property.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from landlords and real-estate investors with growing
property portfolios who need coverage that keeps pace with new
acquisitions. The competitive set includes instant-quote insurtech
landlord-insurance platforms (fast, but no portfolio-level review) and
general personal-lines brokers (broad, but without deep landlord/
multi-property or surplus-lines expertise).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: individual landlords and real-estate
investors who own multiple rental properties and need coverage that
scales with their portfolio, rather than a single-property homeowner
policy.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a portfolio coverage-gap review: every property a
landlord owns is assessed against their current policies before a
consolidated, multi-carrier placement recommendation, including
surplus-lines access for properties standard carriers decline.

## Revenue Architecture

New-business commission is earned at binding, but meaningful renewal
and contingency commission revenue builds gradually as the client book
of multi-property landlords grows year over year.

## Pricing Strategy

Commission-based (10-15% of premium) from admitted carriers on bound
landlord and umbrella policies, paid by the carrier, plus a flat
€200-€500 surplus-lines broker fee for hard-to-place risks placed with
excess and surplus (E&S) carriers.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "coverage that keeps up with your growing property
portfolio." Primary channel is partnerships with local landlord
associations, real-estate-investor meetups, and property management
companies; educational content on multi-property coverage gaps is the
secondary channel, with property-management-software integration
partnerships as an experimental channel.

## Sales System

A portfolio coverage-gap review assesses every property a landlord
owns before recommending a consolidated placement — a roughly 21-day
cycle reflecting the real technical work required to review a
multi-property, multi-carrier account.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: portfolio coverage-gap review and
multi-carrier quote comparison, policy binding and renewal processing,
surplus-lines market access for hard-to-place risks, and claims
advocacy support.

## Technology Stack

Built on Applied Epic as the agency management system, Ivans Exchange
for carrier connectivity and quoting, and Canopy Connect as an
optional client insurance-data aggregation tool.

## Automation Opportunities

Quoting, policy documentation, and renewal reminders are automated
through the agency management system, but coverage-gap review and
complex placement decisions remain fully broker-led.

## Team Structure

Solo-friendly at launch, with an account manager or additional
producer added as the multi-property client book grows.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €12,000-€28,000 covers licensing (including
surplus-lines authority), agency-management-system setup, and carrier
appointment costs — a Medium budget tier; target monthly income ranges
€5,000-€22,000 with an estimated 10-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against portfolio-review-to-bound-placement conversion,
year-over-year policy-renewal rate, gross margin against licensing and
E&O insurance costs, and the consultation-to-placement close rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with low AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are insurtech landlord-insurance platforms threatening disintermediation,
ongoing coverage-gap monitoring exposure as portfolios grow, and
hard-market cycles in property insurance suddenly restricting carrier
appetite.

## Competitive Advantages

Recurring renewal plus contingency commission compounds as the client
book grows, surplus-lines market access lets the brokerage place risks
instant-quote platforms decline, and a growing property portfolio
naturally grows premium volume through the same broker relationship.

## Launch Strategy

Obtain P&C and surplus-lines licensing and set up carrier appointments,
then complete portfolio coverage-gap reviews for the first 10-15
landlord clients, even at a discounted introductory rate.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-6: complete licensing and carrier-appointment setup. Weeks
7-10: complete portfolio reviews for the first 10-15 landlord clients.
Weeks 11-13: standardize the portfolio-review-to-bound-placement
workflow across admitted and surplus-lines carriers.

## Scaling Strategy

Build deep relationships within one local landlord and real-estate-
investor community first, then expand into adjacent geographic markets
or add a commercial-property specialization once carrier appetite and
licensing requirements are confirmed.

## Exit Opportunities

Documented client book, carrier relationships, and renewal history
make this sellable to a larger insurance brokerage or agency
consolidator.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for coverage-gap detection — an implementation
accelerator — while keeping every recommendation manually reviewed
and final placement decisions fully broker-led given real E&O
liability stakes.
