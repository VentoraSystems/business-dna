# Business Blueprint — Loop Chatbot Studio

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an AI customer-support chatbot implementation
agency as a business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25
sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Loop Chatbot Studio is a boutique agency that designs, implements, and
manages AI-powered customer-support chatbots for e-commerce and SaaS
companies, built on established platforms rather than custom software.
It's for someone with real support-operations or no-code AI platform
experience, ready to sell and deliver measurable ticket-resolution
results — a low-capital, fully remote technology-services business.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theBuilder` or `theOperator` archetype, with at least
two years of customer-support operations or AI platform configuration
experience. Comfortable presenting resolution-rate data to skeptical
support leaders rather than deep custom-software engineering.

## Business Overview

An agency configuring and managing AI chatbot deployments (built on
platforms like Intercom Fin) for e-commerce and SaaS clients, delivering
one-time implementation projects that convert into recurring monthly
management retainers.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from support and customer-experience leaders under
pressure to reduce ticket volume and cost without sacrificing quality.
The competitive set includes in-house AI initiatives (slower, requires
internal expertise) and the chatbot platform vendors themselves
(self-serve tools that work but lack hands-on implementation support).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: e-commerce and SaaS companies with real,
visible support-ticket volume who want measurable results without
building an in-house AI team. The core pain point is skepticism about
whether AI support actually resolves tickets or just frustrates
customers.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a scoped pilot implementation targeting a client's
highest-volume ticket category, followed by an ongoing monthly
management and optimization retainer.

## Revenue Architecture

A one-time setup fee is collected upfront at project kickoff, while the
larger recurring management retainer builds gradually as more clients
are retained month over month.

## Pricing Strategy

€2,500-6,000 one-time setup and implementation fee, plus a
€800-2,500/month management retainer based on ticket volume, or
performance-based pricing at €1.50-3 per AI-resolved ticket for larger
clients.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "AI support that actually resolves tickets, not just
deflects them." Primary channel is direct outbound to support leaders at
e-commerce/SaaS companies; published case studies with real
before/after numbers build secondary credibility, with chatbot-platform
partner referrals as an experimental channel.

## Sales System

A discovery call analyzes current ticket volume and categories, followed
by a scoped 4-6 week pilot implementation targeting the highest-volume
ticket types — a roughly 30-day cycle from first inquiry to signed
pilot.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: client onboarding and knowledge-base
ingestion, chatbot flow design and testing, ongoing performance
monitoring and retraining, and monthly resolution-rate reporting.

## Technology Stack

Built on Intercom Fin as the primary chatbot platform, with Zendesk for
helpdesk integration and Airtable for client-reporting dashboards.

## Automation Opportunities

The core deliverable is itself an automation system; client reporting
and conversation-quality monitoring are substantially automated through
the chatbot platform's own analytics.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; the first growth hire is typically a
second implementation specialist once the playbook is documented and a
stable retained-client base is established.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €3,000-€10,000 covers platform licensing, a
demo pilot, and initial sales tooling — a Low budget tier; target
monthly income ranges €8,000-€25,000 with an estimated 9-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against retained-client retention, gross margin against platform
subscription and contractor costs, discovery-call-to-pilot conversion,
and client churn.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with low AI-resistance (a genuine, distinctive
irony for a business built on AI) — the three biggest risks are
dependency on third-party chatbot-platform pricing and API changes,
commoditization as self-serve AI support tools improve, and reputation
damage from an underperforming chatbot deployment.

## Competitive Advantages

Fully remote delivery keeps margin high, the recurring management
retainer smooths cash flow beyond the initial setup fee, and the
business rides a genuine, fast-growing market tailwind toward
AI-assisted customer support.

## Launch Strategy

Choose a primary chatbot platform partner, build a polished demo
implementation as a portfolio piece, then land a first paying pilot
client on their highest-volume, simplest ticket category to prove
resolution-rate ROI.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: choose the platform partner and build a demo implementation.
Weeks 5-9: land and deliver a first paid pilot implementation. Weeks
10-13: publish a case study from the pilot's results and launch outbound
sales with defined service packages.

## Scaling Strategy

Prove ROI with a handful of pilot clients first, refine the
implementation playbook, then hire a second implementation specialist
to take on new client onboarding while the founder focuses on sales.

## Exit Opportunities

Documented client contracts, the implementation playbook, and platform
partnerships make this sellable to another operator or a larger AI
services firm.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for conversation-quality monitoring and client
reporting — implementation accelerators — while keeping the core
implementation and account-strategy work founder-led.
