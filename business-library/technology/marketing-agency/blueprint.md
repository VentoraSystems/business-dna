# Business Blueprint — Fernway Marketing Group

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow a solo-led full-funnel marketing agency as a business.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Fernway Marketing Group is a full-funnel demand-generation agency for
seed-to-Series-A B2B SaaS founders who need real marketing execution —
content, paid, and lifecycle — but can't yet justify a $15k/month
agency-of-record retainer or a full in-house hire. Unlike the
technology-services agencies in this library, Fernway's revenue is a
genuine monthly retainer from day one, not a project fee with an optional
add-on. It's for someone who can run a real funnel-audit conversation and
turn it into a scoped, priced retainer within a single call.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theConnector` or `theHustler` archetype, with at least
four years of hands-on marketing execution experience (not just strategy).
The strongest `creator` and `seller` archetype match in this category —
this business rewards someone who can both produce compelling content and
close a recurring retainer conversation.

## Business Overview

A lean, full-funnel marketing agency serving venture-backed B2B SaaS
companies between seed and Series A, delivering content, paid acquisition,
and lifecycle marketing under a single monthly retainer with a freelancer
bench for execution capacity.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from founders who raised a round, have a growth mandate from
their board, and no marketing hire yet — or just lost one. The competitive
set includes full-service agencies-of-record (priced out of reach at this
stage) and generalist freelancers (lack the full-funnel coordination a
board update requires).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: seed-to-Series-A SaaS founders and their
first growth or marketing hire. Common pain points are a board asking for
a pipeline number nobody owns, and marketing spend that's scattered across
freelancers with no single throughline.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a monthly full-funnel retainer (content, paid, lifecycle
under one roof), always opened with a free 30-minute funnel audit, with an
optional one-time launch-sprint project fee for clients who need a
compressed pre-launch push before the retainer begins.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is a genuine monthly retainer from the first signed client, not a
project fee with an optional retainer bolted on — the defining difference
from every technology-services business in this library. A minimum
three-month commitment protects against premature client judgment before
a funnel has time to compound.

## Pricing Strategy

Tiered monthly retainer pricing scoped during the free funnel audit, with
a required three-month minimum commitment — this protects margin from the
single biggest risk in this business: clients judging results before a
full funnel cycle has run.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "the marketing team your Series A budget can actually
afford." Primary channel is founder-network referrals from happy clients;
published case studies with named metrics and LinkedIn thought leadership
build secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

A free 30-minute funnel audit reviews the founder's current channels and
board-level growth targets, converting into a scoped retainer proposal
within the same week — a roughly 21-day cycle from first conversation to
signed retainer.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: a monthly funnel audit, content-calendar
sprints, a weekly reporting call, and a quarterly strategy reset — run by
a solo strategist coordinating a freelancer bench for design, copy, and ad
execution.

## Technology Stack

Built on HubSpot for CRM and lifecycle automation, Google Ads and Meta Ads
Manager for paid execution, and AI tools for first-draft content and
campaign-optimization suggestions — always reviewed by the strategist
before anything reaches a client.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted content drafting accelerates the content-calendar sprint;
AI-assisted campaign-optimization suggestions flag underperforming ad
sets faster than manual weekly review alone, always verified by the
strategist before budget is reallocated.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly at launch, powered by a freelancer bench for
design and copy capacity; a second in-house strategist is typically the
first hire once five or more clients are on stable retainers.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €4,000-€9,000 covers CRM and automation setup,
SEO and analytics tooling, and a portfolio site; target monthly income
ranges €6,000-€18,000 with an estimated 5-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against funnel-audit-to-signed-retainer close rate, retainer
customer retention past the three-month minimum, monthly recurring
revenue growth, and gross margin.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and low AI-resistance for the basic-copywriting layer
of the work specifically — the three biggest risks are marketing budgets
getting cut first in a downturn regardless of campaign performance,
ROI attribution across multi-channel campaigns being genuinely hard to
prove, and AI content tools commoditizing basic copywriting over time.

## Competitive Advantages

A free funnel audit that makes the sales conversation concrete instead of
abstract, named case studies with real metrics that build trust faster
than generic agency claims, and a three-month minimum commitment that
protects both sides from premature judgment.

## Launch Strategy

Set up CRM, ad accounts, and analytics tooling, build 2-3 anonymized case
studies from prior freelance work as portfolio proof, then sell the first
retainer through a free funnel audit.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-3: finish tooling setup and assemble the freelancer bench. Weeks
4-9: run funnel audits and onboard the first two retainer clients, running
the first monthly content-calendar sprint. Weeks 10-13: deliver the first
full reporting cycle and publish the first named case study.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by productizing the "launch sprint" as a standalone entry offer that
feeds the retainer, then bring on a second in-house strategist once five
or more clients are on stable multi-month retainers.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client roster of stable multi-month retainers, named case
studies, and a repeatable funnel-audit sales process are attractive to a
larger agency looking to acquire a proven client book and delivery
process rather than build one from scratch.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for first-draft content and campaign-optimization
flags — implementation accelerators — while keeping strategy, client
relationships, and final creative judgment fully human-led.
