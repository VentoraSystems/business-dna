# Business Blueprint — Ember & Oak

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run a full-service scratch-kitchen restaurant as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Ember & Oak is a full-service, scratch-kitchen neighborhood restaurant
built around a seasonal New American dinner menu and a full bar program,
serving a planned dining-out occasion — date nights, celebrations, a
reliable weekend go-to spot. It's for a hands-on operator with real
kitchen or restaurant-management experience, ready to lead a much larger
team through notoriously demanding hours — the highest capital and
staffing complexity of the two physical food-service businesses in this
library.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theOperator` or `theVisionary` archetype, requiring at
least three years of real kitchen or restaurant-management experience.
This package assumes culinary and hospitality-management competence are
already in place — it covers the business side only.

## Business Overview

A full-service restaurant delivering a seasonal dinner menu and full bar
program, led by a chef and supported by a front-of-house and
back-of-house team, growing through reservation-platform visibility,
local press, and private-event and catering bookings.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from local diners seeking a reliable dining-out destination
for planned occasions. The competitive set includes fast-casual and
chain restaurants (cheaper, faster, less distinctive) and other
independent scratch-kitchen restaurants competing for the same
reservation-based dining occasion.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: local diners aged 30-55 celebrating an
occasion or seeking a reliable "go-to" dinner spot, plus date-night
couples. The core need is a dependable, worth-the-reservation dining
experience distinct from both fast-casual convenience and formal
fine dining.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a seasonal dinner menu with a full bar program, plus
private-event and catering bookings as a premium add-on revenue stream
for celebrations and business events.

## Revenue Architecture

Card and cash payments settle at time of service like a coffee shop, but
the reservation-based, slower table-turn dynamic makes revenue timing
less immediate and less predictable than counter service.

## Pricing Strategy

Per-cover average check of €45-65 including drinks (entrées €22-32),
plus a €1,500 minimum spend for private dining-room event bookings.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "seasonal, scratch-made, and worth the reservation."
Primary channel is reservation-platform listing and local food-press
visibility; seasonal-menu social content builds secondary awareness,
with private-event and catering outreach as an experimental revenue
channel.

## Sales System

A prospective diner books a table through a reservation platform or
phone call and arrives for a seated dinner experience — a short,
planned-occasion cycle, distinct from a coffee shop's instant impulse
purchase.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: daily prep and mise en place, dinner service
execution across front-of-house and back-of-house, reservation and table
management, and vendor ordering with food-safety compliance checks.

## Technology Stack

Built on Toast for point-of-sale and kitchen display, OpenTable for
reservations and table management, with MarketMan as an optional
inventory and purchasing tool.

## Automation Opportunities

Reservation confirmations and inventory-reorder alerts are automated;
AI-assisted purchasing forecasts suggest ingredient order quantities to
reduce food waste.

## Team Structure

Not solo-friendly — requires a chef, line cooks, servers, a host, and a
dishwasher from day one; the first growth hire is typically a sous chef
or kitchen manager to reduce the founder's nightly hands-on load.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €30,000-€75,000 covers commercial kitchen
equipment, dining-room and bar buildout, and liquor licensing — a High
budget tier; target monthly income ranges €35,000-€90,000 with an
estimated 18-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against repeat-reservation retention, gross margin against food/
beverage cost of goods and labor, reservation-platform and press
conversion, and repeat-diner attrition.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty with high AI-resistance — scratch cooking and hands-on
hospitality require a skilled in-person team. The three biggest risks
are notoriously thin restaurant-industry margins, a high fixed-labor
break-even revenue floor, and the added regulatory lead time of liquor
licensing on top of food-safety compliance.

## Competitive Advantages

Private-event and catering bookings diversify revenue beyond nightly
covers, a full bar program adds high-margin revenue beyond food cost of
goods, and a reservation-based model creates a predictable weekend
revenue floor.

## Launch Strategy

Secure High-tier financing and complete kitchen/dining-room buildout,
start liquor licensing early given its long lead time, then validate the
menu concept through pop-up dinners before committing to the full lease.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-8: finalize buildout, licensing, and permitting. Weeks 9-11: run
a soft opening at reduced capacity. Weeks 12-13: launch the full public
opening with local food-press outreach and a reservation-platform
listing.

## Scaling Strategy

Build a stable base of weekend reservation volume and weekday covers
first, grow private-event and catering bookings as incremental revenue,
then add a sous chef or kitchen manager before considering a second
location.

## Exit Opportunities

Documented lease terms, equipment, recipes/menu, and staff structure
make this sellable to another operator — though exit activity carries
more capital and staffing complexity to transfer than a coffee shop.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for purchasing forecasts and reservation management
— implementation accelerators — while keeping every dish cooked and
every table served fully staff-led.
