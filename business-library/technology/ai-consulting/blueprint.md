# Business Blueprint — Grayridge AI Advisory

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow an independent AI strategy advisory as a business. Each
`##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s
v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Grayridge AI Advisory is an independent, vendor-neutral AI strategy
advisory: a solo principal advisor sells a fixed-fee, 2-4 week AI-readiness
diagnostic sprint to mid-market leadership teams, producing a
prioritized, defensible roadmap of which AI opportunities are worth
pursuing and which aren't. It explicitly does not implement anything —
no code, no engineering, no software delivered. It's for someone with
genuine cross-industry AI depth and executive credibility. It is not a
fit for someone who wants to build things — that's `ai-automation-agency`
or `software-house`, two different businesses in this same
`technology-services` category that this advisory can refer clients to
for the build phase.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theVisionary` or `theSpecialist` archetype, with at least
six years of experience and enough executive-level credibility that a
COO will trust an outside opinion over their own team's enthusiasm for a
pet AI project.

## Business Overview

A boutique, principal-led AI strategy advisory serving mid-market
companies (200-2,000 employees) that feel pressure to "do something with
AI" but lack a clear, defensible framework for deciding what.

## Market Intelligence

Demand is driven by executive anxiety about falling behind on AI without
a rigorous way to prioritize competing initiatives. The competitive set
ranges from big-name management consultancies (slow, expensive, and often
staffed by generalists) to AI implementation agencies with an inherent
conflict of interest (they profit from recommending a build). An
independent, vendor-neutral boutique sits deliberately outside both.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: COOs and CTOs at mid-market companies under
pressure to show AI progress. The common pain point is a leadership team
with several competing AI ideas and no rigorous way to rank them before
committing real engineering budget.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-fee diagnostic sprint — stakeholder interviews,
an opportunity-scoring workshop, and a written, prioritized roadmap —
with an optional monthly advisory retainer for implementation oversight
once the client proceeds.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue arrives as discrete diagnostic-sprint sales rather than a smooth
ramp, supplemented by a smaller, slower-building advisory-retainer layer.

## Pricing Strategy

A fixed-fee diagnostic sprint gives leadership budget certainty upfront,
which a time-and-materials consulting engagement can't offer, and signals
confidence in the framework's efficiency.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "know exactly where AI will and won't move the needle —
before you spend a cent building it." Primary channel is referrals
through the founder's own executive network; thought-leadership content
and conference speaking build secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

Executive-level consultative selling — a single diagnostic-scoping
conversation directly with the COO or CTO, with a roughly 35-day cycle
from first conversation to signed sprint.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: stakeholder-interview rounds, an
opportunity-scoring workshop with leadership, a written roadmap
deliverable, and optional ongoing implementation-oversight advisory.

## Technology Stack

Built on Notion for engagement documentation and a proprietary,
vendor-neutral AI opportunity-scoring matrix — deliberately low
automation, since the firm's product is judgment, not software.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted competitive-intelligence monitoring keeps every diagnostic
current against a fast-moving vendor and model landscape without
requiring manual research from scratch each time.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly — a second senior advisor is typically the
first hire once diagnostic-sprint demand exceeds what one principal can
personally deliver with full credibility.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €8,000-€25,000 covers positioning, legal setup,
and research subscriptions; target monthly income ranges €12,000-€35,000
with an estimated 6-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against diagnostic-scoping-call close rate, gross margin,
advisory-retainer conversion (customer retention), and retainer MRR
growth.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty and — notably — high AI-resistance, despite the subject
matter being AI itself: the firm's actual product is independent human
judgment and credibility, which AI tools can't replicate. The two biggest
risks are being dismissed as "just PowerPoint consulting" and scope
confusion with prospects who actually want an implementation, not a
strategy.

## Competitive Advantages

Explicit vendor-neutral positioning, a fixed-fee structure that gives
leadership budget certainty, and a structured opportunity-scoring
framework that turns a vague AI mandate into a ranked, defensible
roadmap.

## Launch Strategy

Build and pilot the opportunity-scoring framework internally, then sell
the first diagnostic sprint through a personal network referral rather
than cold outreach — credibility, not volume, opens the first door.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finalize the opportunity-scoring framework and the
diagnostic-sprint proposal template. Weeks 5-9: sell and deliver the
first diagnostic sprint through a trusted referral, holding scope firmly
to diagnosis rather than drifting into implementation. Weeks 10-13:
publish the first vendor-neutral thought-leadership piece and ask the
first client for one warm introduction to a peer executive.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by bringing on a second senior advisor to run parallel diagnostic
sprints, then package the opportunity-scoring framework into a
licensable methodology once it's been proven across at least ten
independent engagements.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented opportunity-scoring framework, a portfolio of client
roadmaps, and executive-network relationships are attractive to a larger
advisory or consulting firm looking to acquire a proven, differentiated
methodology.

## AI Recommendations

Use AI to accelerate research and competitive-intelligence monitoring,
never to generate the actual prioritization judgment — the entire value
proposition rests on independent, defensible human reasoning that a
client trusts precisely because it isn't automated.
