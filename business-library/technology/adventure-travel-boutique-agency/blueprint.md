# Business Blueprint — Ridge & Range Adventures

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run a boutique adventure-travel agency as a business.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Ridge & Range Adventures is a boutique travel agency specializing in
curated small-group and custom adventure trips for active travelers
aged 30-55, built on the founder's own firsthand trip experience and a
trusted guide network rather than a generic booking-platform catalog.
It's for someone with genuine adventure-travel experience, comfortable
with frequent personal travel to scout destinations — a meaningfully
different lifestyle profile from the desk-based businesses elsewhere in
this library.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theConnector` or `theSpecialist` archetype, with at
least two years of firsthand adventure-travel experience and a real
network of trusted local guides and operators.

## Business Overview

A founder-led boutique agency curating adventure trips through personal
firsthand knowledge and a vetted guide network, delivering both custom
independent itineraries and founder-led small-group departures.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from active travelers who want genuine destination
expertise rather than a generic booking-platform search. The
competitive set includes self-service booking platforms (cheaper, no
personal expertise) and mass-market travel agencies (broader but less
adventure-specialized).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: active travelers aged 30-55 planning
multi-day trekking, hiking, or adventure-sports trips who want real
destination expertise and trusted on-the-ground guides. The core need
is confidence that the trip will actually deliver on its promise.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is custom independent-trip itinerary planning and
founder-led small-group adventure departures, both built on the
founder's own firsthand destination and guide-network knowledge.

## Revenue Architecture

Clients pay a deposit at booking and the balance before departure, but
supplier commissions are often only paid out after the trip is
completed, slowing overall cash collection relative to a retail
transaction.

## Pricing Strategy

€300-800 custom independent-trip itinerary planning fee, or 15-20%
commission/markup built into founder-led small-group trips priced at
€2,500-6,000 per person for an 8-14 day guided adventure.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "adventure, planned by people who've actually done it."
Primary channel is content marketing from the founder's own firsthand
trip experience; a past-client referral program builds secondary
growth, with outdoor-gear brand affiliate partnerships as an
experimental channel.

## Sales System

A discovery call explores the prospective client's fitness level,
travel style, and destination interests, followed by a custom
itinerary proposal — a roughly 45-day cycle, longer than most consumer
services given the trip's cost and duration.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: destination and guide-network vetting through
personal scouting, custom itinerary design and supplier coordination,
pre-trip client preparation, and on-trip support for founder-led group
departures.

## Technology Stack

Built on Travefy for itinerary planning, with WeTravel for group-trip
payments and booking and Later for social content scheduling.

## Automation Opportunities

Itinerary templates and booking confirmations are partially automated;
AI-assisted first-draft itinerary generation speeds up planning, refined
and finalized by the founder's own destination expertise.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; the first growth hire is typically a
second trip planner or trip leader who has personally traveled the
routes they'll lead.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €10,000-€30,000 covers booking software,
scouting travel, and insurance — a Medium budget tier, driven largely by
the founder's own scouting travel; target monthly income ranges
€6,000-€18,000 with an estimated 10-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against discovery-call-to-booking conversion, repeat/referral
client retention, gross margin against scouting and content-production
costs, and repeat-client attrition.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with moderate AI-resistance — the three biggest
risks are unreliable on-the-ground guide or operator partners, external
shocks halting bookings for entire regions, and low barriers to entry
from self-service booking platforms.

## Competitive Advantages

Genuine firsthand destination expertise is hard for generic platforms
to replicate, high average trip value means meaningful commission per
booking, and repeat/referral-driven relationships build a durable book
of business over time.

## Launch Strategy

Build the initial trusted guide network through personal scouting
travel, secure registration and insurance, then sell and lead a small
pilot group trip to prove itinerary quality before scaling marketing.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-6: complete initial scouting travel and secure registration and
insurance. Weeks 7-10: sell and prepare the pilot group trip. Weeks
11-13: lead the pilot trip and publish firsthand trip content from it.

## Scaling Strategy

Build a stable repeat/referral client base across a couple of signature
trip types first, launch a signature annual small-group trip as an
anchor offering, then bring on a second trip planner or leader.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented guide and supplier network plus client book make this
sellable to another adventure-travel operator or boutique agency group.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for first-draft itinerary generation — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping destination curation and
guide vetting fully founder-led.
