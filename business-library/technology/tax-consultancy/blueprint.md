# Business Blueprint — Ledgerline Tax Advisors

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a tax-preparation practice as a business — it is not tax
advice for any end client, and should never be used as a substitute for
advice from a licensed tax professional given to a specific person or
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Ledgerline Tax Advisors is a seasonal individual and small-business tax
preparation practice built around an Enrolled Agent credential. The
business snapshot: an EA-credentialed preparer files individual and
business tax returns for a flat, complexity-tiered fee, with revenue
concentrated in a few months of the year, then works to convert a
portion of that seasonal client base into year-round tax-planning
retainers. It's for a detail-driven specialist comfortable with intense
seasonal deadline pressure, not someone who needs steady, evenly-paced
work.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theOperator` archetype — someone who
enjoys the technical puzzle of the tax code and thrives under a hard
deadline. Three-plus years of prior tax-preparation experience
meaningfully shortens the ramp-up to handling complex returns
confidently.

## Business Overview

A seasonal tax-preparation practice for individuals and small
businesses, positioned on Enrolled Agent credentialing and the ability
to represent clients directly before the tax authority.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for tax preparation is stable and recurring, but increasingly
pressured at the simple end by self-service and AI-assisted software;
the competitive landscape ranges from national franchise chains to
independent EAs and CPAs.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are self-employed individuals with moderately complex
returns and small-business owners needing annual filing. Their core
pain point is worrying they're missing deductions they're entitled to.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is flat-fee individual and business tax-return
preparation and e-filing, tiered by complexity, with a year-round
tax-planning retainer available for business clients.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is sharply seasonal — concentrated in a few months around
filing deadlines — smoothed only as year-round business retainer
clients grow.

## Pricing Strategy

Flat fee per return-complexity tier for individuals; a retainer
structure for business clients needing year-round planning.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "every deduction found, every deadline met." Primary
channel is referrals from existing clients and local accountants; local
SEO for "tax preparer near me" builds secondary demand.

## Sales System

A short, largely transactional first engagement (roughly 14 days) that
converts into a repeat, once-a-year relationship for most clients.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: document collection, return preparation and
review, e-filing, and year-round planning check-ins for retainer
clients.

## Technology Stack

Built on Drake Tax, Lacerte, or UltraTax CS as the core professional
tax-preparation and e-filing platform.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted document data-extraction and deduction-finding meaningfully
speed up return preparation during the highest-pressure weeks of the
season.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; seasonal contract preparers are
typically the first hire to expand peak-season capacity.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €6,000-€20,000 covers Enrolled Agent
credentialing and professional tax software; target monthly income
ranges €4,000-€16,000 (concentrated seasonally) with an estimated
6-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against total annual revenue, gross margin, referral-lead
conversion, and year-over-year client return rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and low AI-resistance — the biggest risk is
self-service and AI-assisted software automating the simplest, most
price-sensitive returns.

## Competitive Advantages

Enrolled Agent credential allowing direct tax-authority representation,
fast e-file turnaround, and transparent flat per-complexity-tier
pricing.

## Launch Strategy

Get EA-credentialed first, then start marketing at least 8 weeks before
the season begins — launching too close to the filing deadline leaves
no time to convert new clients.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish EA credentialing and set up professional tax
software. Weeks 5-8: prepare returns for the first 10-15 clients,
timing each one to calibrate pricing. Weeks 9-13: standardize the
document-intake checklist and launch local marketing ahead of the next
season.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by converting a portion of seasonal individual clients into
year-round tax-planning retainer clients, then adding seasonal contract
preparers to expand peak-season capacity.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented client list and seasonal workflow are attractive to
larger tax practices looking to acquire a book of recurring seasonal
clients.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for document extraction and deduction-finding, not
as a replacement for final review — the EA's signature and
representation rights remain the core value.
