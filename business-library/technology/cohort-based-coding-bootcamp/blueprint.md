# Business Blueprint — Foundry Code School

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run a cohort-based coding bootcamp as a business.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Foundry Code School is a live, instructor-led, cohort-based full-stack
web development bootcamp for career changers, delivered in small
cohorts with structured accountability and job-placement support —
the first business authored under `online-course-platforms`,
deliberately distinct from the self-paced content platforms this
category will eventually include.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theBuilder` or `theSpecialist` archetype, with at
least five years of relevant experience. Not solo-friendly — running
live cohort instruction plus dedicated career-services support
requires a small team of instructors and career coaches from day one.

## Business Overview

A live-instruction education service, not a content library: students
join a fixed-schedule cohort of 20-30 peers, follow a structured
project-based curriculum with a lead instructor and teaching
assistants, and receive dedicated career coaching through to job
placement.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from career changers seeking a structured, accountable
path into software engineering. The competitive set includes
self-paced online course platforms (cheaper, but far lower completion
rates) and university computer-science degrees (more credentialed, but
far slower and more expensive).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: career changers, typically aged 25-40,
seeking a structured path into software engineering with real
accountability and job-placement support.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-schedule, live cohort: 12 weeks full-time or
24 weeks part-time, project-based curriculum, teaching-assistant
support, and dedicated career coaching through to job placement.

## Revenue Architecture

Upfront-tuition cohorts collect revenue quickly at enrollment, but
income-share-agreement (ISA) revenue is delayed and spread over 24
months after graduates land a qualifying job, producing a mixed
collection pattern.

## Pricing Strategy

€11,000 upfront tuition for the 12-week full-time immersive cohort, a
10%-of-salary-for-24-months ISA capped at €15,000 as an alternative,
or €6,500 for the 24-week part-time evenings-and-weekends track.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "learn to code with 30 classmates, not 3,000 strangers."
Primary channel is published, verifiable job-placement outcomes and
career-changer testimonials; coding-community and meetup partnerships
are the secondary channel, with corporate re-skilling partner
referrals as an experimental channel.

## Sales System

An admissions process including a coding-aptitude assessment and
interview qualifies applicants before they commit to a cohort — a
roughly 30-day cycle, longer than a self-serve course purchase given
the size of the tuition decision.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: applicant screening and admissions
interviews, live cohort instruction and project review, career-
services coaching and hiring-partner matching, and post-graduation
outcomes tracking.

## Technology Stack

Built on Canvas LMS for the learning-management system, Zoom for live
cohort instruction, and GitHub Classroom as an optional code-review
and assignment-management tool.

## Automation Opportunities

Admissions scheduling and course-material distribution are automated
through the learning-management system, but live instruction,
mentorship, and career coaching remain fully human-led by design.

## Team Structure

Not solo-friendly — requires a small team of instructors, teaching
assistants, and career coaches from day one to run live cohort
instruction at the quality bar students expect.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €20,000-€45,000 covers curriculum
development, learning-management-system setup, and admissions/
marketing launch costs — a Medium budget tier; target monthly income
ranges €15,000-€45,000 with an estimated 14-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against applicant-to-enrollment conversion, cohort completion
rate, gross margin against instructor and career-coach costs, and the
admissions-interview-to-enrollment close rate.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty with moderate AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are AI coding assistants lowering the perceived necessity of
structured instruction, the cohort format's hard cap on enrollment
scale per cohort, and job-placement outcomes being directly exposed to
tech-hiring-market cyclicality.

## Competitive Advantages

Live cohort accountability produces meaningfully higher completion
rates than self-paced courses, published verifiable job-placement
outcomes build a genuine trust moat, and the ISA option removes the
upfront-cost barrier for career changers who can't afford full tuition.

## Launch Strategy

Design the full-stack curriculum around AI-augmented development
workflows, then run a discounted pilot cohort of 10-15 students to
validate curriculum pacing and completion before the first full-price
cohort.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-6: design the curriculum and set up the learning-management
system. Weeks 7-10: run a discounted pilot cohort and track completion
and satisfaction closely. Weeks 11-13: complete the first full-price
cohort end-to-end, including career-services support for graduates.

## Scaling Strategy

Fill each cohort to consistent capacity with strong outcomes first,
then increase cohort frequency in step with hiring-partner
relationship growth, before adding a second track or specialization.

## Exit Opportunities

Documented curriculum, hiring-partner network, and job-placement track
record make this sellable to a larger education company, corporate
training provider, or another bootcamp operator.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for applicant-screening assistance — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping the curriculum itself
focused on teaching AI-augmented development skills as core content,
not treating AI purely as an operational efficiency tool.
