# Business Blueprint — Fairview Legal Partners

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a boutique business-law practice as a business — it is not
legal advice for any end client, and should never be used as a substitute for
advice from a licensed attorney given to a specific person or business. Each
`##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s
v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Fairview Legal Partners is a boutique business-law practice serving
startups and small companies with contracts, formation documents, and
ongoing general-counsel retainers. The business snapshot: a bar-certified
attorney drafts and reviews documents for a flat fee or hourly rate, and
grows toward a base of monthly general-counsel retainer clients. It's for
a detail-rigorous specialist comfortable carrying professional liability,
not someone seeking fast, low-friction scale.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theOperator` archetype — someone who
finds precision drafting satisfying and communicates with the kind of
careful negotiation skill that reassures nervous first-time founders.
Four-plus years of prior legal practice meaningfully shortens the
ramp-up to independent judgment.

## Business Overview

A boutique business-law practice positioned on flat-fee predictability
and bar-certified reliability, serving companies too small for a
full-time in-house counsel.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for accessible business-law services is steady as more startups
form each year; the competitive landscape includes large full-service
firms (expensive, slow for small matters) and unreviewed online
templates (cheap, legally risky).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are startups and small businesses (2-50 employees).
Their core pain point is using unreviewed online contract templates
that may not hold up if disputed.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is flat-fee standardized documents (formation, contract
templates) plus hourly billing for negotiated matters, with a
general-counsel retainer available for ongoing needs.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue blends fast-cash flat-fee document work with a slower-building,
more durable general-counsel retainer base.

## Pricing Strategy

Hourly for complex or negotiated matters, flat fee for standardized
documents, monthly retainer for ongoing general counsel.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "contracts that hold up, not just contracts that close."
Primary channel is referrals from accountants, business consultants, and
existing clients; practical content builds secondary awareness.

## Sales System

A consultative sale — a free initial consultation and conflict check is
the standard first step, with a roughly 21-day sales cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: initial consultation and conflict check,
document drafting and review, contract negotiation support, and
quarterly general-counsel check-ins.

## Technology Stack

Built on Clio for practice management and LawPay for trust-accounting-
compliant billing.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted first-pass contract review and legal research speed up
drafting, always followed by full attorney review before anything goes
to a client.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; an associate attorney is typically the
first hire once billable capacity is consistently maxed.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €15,000-€45,000 covers malpractice insurance,
practice-management software, and office setup; target monthly income
ranges €6,000-€22,000 with an estimated 10-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against annual revenue, gross margin, general-counsel retainer
retention, and consultation-to-engagement close rate.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty and high AI-resistance. The two biggest risks are
professional malpractice liability and a hard revenue ceiling tied to
billable hours until a second attorney joins.

## Competitive Advantages

Bar-certified with full malpractice coverage, flat-fee document
packages that remove pricing uncertainty, and fast contract turnaround.

## Launch Strategy

Get malpractice insurance and trust-accounting software active first,
then activate the referral network of accountants and consultants — the
highest-trust introduction source, not cold advertising.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish insurance and software setup. Weeks 5-8: complete the
first 5-10 flat-fee document engagements, always running a conflict
check first. Weeks 9-13: standardize the document-template library and
announce the practice to the referral network.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by converting one-off transactional clients into ongoing
general-counsel retainers, then adding an associate attorney once
billable capacity is consistently maxed.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented client book with standardized matter files is
attractive to larger firms looking to acquire a book of general-counsel
retainer relationships.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for first-pass contract review and legal research,
never as a replacement for final attorney judgment.
