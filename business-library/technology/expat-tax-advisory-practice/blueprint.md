# Business Blueprint — Meridian Expat Tax

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an expat tax advisory practice as a business.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure. Nothing here is tax,
legal, or compliance advice for any end client._

## Executive Summary

Meridian is a fully remote tax-advisory practice specializing in
cross-border compliance for individuals living outside their country
of tax residency, built on Enrolled Agent credentialing plus
additional FATCA-compliance training — deliberately differentiated
from the domestic `tax-consultancy` elsewhere in this category by its
cross-border, year-round niche rather than a seasonal, single-country
practice.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theConnector` archetype, with at
least four years of relevant experience. Solo-friendly at launch —
unlike most of this batch's other businesses, the practice can start
as a single credentialed specialist serving clients entirely remotely.

## Business Overview

A tax-advisory practice serving expatriates, digital nomads, and
cross-border remote workers who must file returns in more than one
country, handling filing-obligation assessment, dual-country return
preparation, and ongoing FBAR/FATCA compliance.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from individuals living outside their country of tax
residency who face dual-country filing obligations. The competitive
set includes generalist domestic tax preparers (who often decline or
mishandle cross-border cases) and large international accounting
firms (accurate but priced well beyond an individual client's budget).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: individuals with cross-border tax
obligations, most commonly citizens living abroad who must file in
both their home and host country, plus a smaller base of digital
nomads and cross-border remote workers.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a filing-obligation assessment followed by
dual-country return preparation, with FBAR/FATCA and host-country
e-filing handled through a secure client portal.

## Revenue Architecture

Unlike a purely domestic tax practice, client deadlines are spread
across the calendar year by country rather than concentrated in a
single season, producing a steadier, less peaked revenue pattern.

## Pricing Strategy

Annual dual-country return preparation from €450-€900 depending on
complexity, a one-time streamlined delinquent-filing package for
€1,200-€2,500, and an annual dual-country compliance retainer at
€900-€1,800/year for ongoing clients.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "compliant in two countries, at home in neither's
paperwork." Primary channel is partnerships with relocation firms and
expat community forums; deadline-focused educational content is the
secondary channel, with digital-nomad-visa program partnerships as an
experimental channel.

## Sales System

A free initial consultation assesses which countries and filing
obligations apply before quoting a flat fee, building the trust
needed for a high-stakes compliance engagement — a roughly 21-day
cycle, longer than a domestic tax practice's, given the real education
prospective clients need about their obligations.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: cross-border filing-obligation assessment,
dual-country return preparation with double-checked internal review,
FBAR/FATCA and host-country e-filing, and ongoing tax-treaty research
maintenance.

## Technology Stack

Built on Drake Tax as the professional tax-preparation software,
TaxDome as the secure client portal and workflow system for a globally
distributed client base, and CCH AnswerConnect as an optional
tax-treaty and cross-border research tool.

## Automation Opportunities

Document intake and e-filing status tracking are automated through the
client portal, but treaty research and final return review remain
fully specialist-led given the liability stakes.

## Team Structure

Solo-friendly at launch, with contract preparers added by
country-pair specialization as the client base grows.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €10,000-€25,000 covers Enrolled Agent
credentialing plus cross-border training, multi-jurisdiction tax
software, and a secure client portal — a Medium budget tier; target
monthly income ranges €5,000-€18,000 with an estimated 8-month path
to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against annual revenue growth, gross margin against research
and liability-insurance costs, free-consultation-to-engagement
conversion, and annual-retainer client renewal rate.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty with moderate AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are continuously evolving tax treaties and totalization agreements
across every jurisdiction served, severe FBAR/FATCA non-compliance
penalty exposure for clients, and currency/time-zone coordination
friction from a globally distributed client base.

## Competitive Advantages

Deadlines spread across the calendar year produce steadier revenue
than a domestic practice's peak season, the Enrolled Agent credential
plus documented cross-border training gives real standing on
high-stakes compliance that generalist preparers avoid, and a fully
remote client base removes the geographic ceiling a local practice
faces.

## Launch Strategy

Obtain Enrolled Agent credentialing plus cross-border/FATCA-compliance
training, then master one high-demand country pair before expanding to
additional jurisdictions.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-6: complete credentialing and cross-border training, and set
up multi-jurisdiction tax software and the secure client portal.
Weeks 7-10: prepare dual-country returns for the first 5-10 clients
within the chosen country-pair specialization. Weeks 11-13: announce
availability within expat community forums and relocation-firm
partnership channels.

## Scaling Strategy

Master one country pair to a stable, profitable client base first,
then add contract preparers by country-pair specialization only after
confirming genuine, sustained demand for each additional jurisdiction.

## Exit Opportunities

Documented country-pair research, compliance-review workflows, and
client relationships make this sellable to a larger cross-border tax
practice or international accounting firm.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for tax-treaty and rule research — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping every result manually
verified and final return review fully specialist-led given the real
liability stakes.
