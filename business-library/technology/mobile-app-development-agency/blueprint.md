# Business Blueprint — Copperline Mobile Studio

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a mobile app development studio as a business. Each `##`
heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2
canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Copperline Mobile Studio is a mobile app development studio: a small team
of platform specialists (iOS, Android, or cross-platform) builds native
and cross-platform apps for funded startups and enterprises on fixed-scope
per-platform contracts, with a dedicated release manager handling App
Store and Play Store submission. It's for a technically credible operator
who has personally been through app-store review before. It is not a fit
for a generalist software house — mobile has its own specific risks (app
store rejection, device fragmentation, OS-version compatibility) that
`software-house`, a different business in this same `technology-services`
category, doesn't specialize in.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theBuilder` or `theOperator` archetype, with at least four
years of mobile-specific experience — prior scar tissue from an app store
rejection is what makes timeline estimates credible to an anxious client.

## Business Overview

A fixed-scope mobile app development studio serving funded startup
founders and enterprise product teams who need a native or cross-platform
iOS/Android app built by specialists, not a generalist web shop.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from two buyer types: consumer-facing startups building
their first mobile product, and enterprises needing an internal
field-operations or customer-facing app. The competitive set includes
generalist software houses (weaker on platform-specific risk) and
low-cost offshore mobile shops (weaker on app-store approval track
record) — a specialist studio with a visible portfolio of approved,
live apps sits between the two.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: funded startup founders and enterprise
product or operations leaders. Common pain points are a previous app
rejected by App Store review with no clear path to resubmission, and
underestimating the ongoing OS-compatibility maintenance a live app
requires.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-scope, per-platform app build, always including
a technical scoping phase that confirms the native-vs-cross-platform
decision, followed by an optional OS-compatibility maintenance retainer.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is project-fee-based with a seasonal bump in maintenance work
around major annual iOS/Android releases, supplemented by a growing
maintenance-retainer base.

## Pricing Strategy

Fixed-scope pricing per platform, set only after a technical scoping call
confirms hardware-dependent features and the native-vs-cross-platform
decision — this protects margin from the framework-limitation risk unique
to mobile.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "native apps that pass App Store review the first time."
Primary proof point is a portfolio of live, published apps with visible
store ratings; founder-network referrals and mobile-dev conference
sponsorship build secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

A technical scoping call reviews the client's spec against platform
constraints before any fixed price is quoted, with a roughly 40-day cycle
from first conversation to signed project.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: platform-specific technical scoping, two-week
sprints with TestFlight/internal-testing-track builds, app-store
submission and review management, and post-launch OS-compatibility
maintenance.

## Technology Stack

Built on Xcode and Android Studio as the core native toolchains, with
Flutter available for cross-platform projects, and Firebase Crashlytics
for crash monitoring across the device matrix.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted crash-log triage prioritizes bug fixes across a fragmented
device and OS-version matrix; AI-assisted code review is an emerging
practice for catching platform-specific anti-patterns before submission.

## Team Structure

Not solo-founder-friendly — a minimum viable team is one iOS specialist,
one Android specialist (or a cross-platform lead), and a release manager
handling app-store communication.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €15,000-€30,000 covers developer accounts,
device-lab hardware, and an initial contractor retainer; target monthly
income ranges €16,000-€42,000 with an estimated 10-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against scoping-call close rate, gross margin per project,
maintenance-retainer conversion (customer retention), and retainer MRR
growth.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty and moderate AI-resistance. The three biggest risks are
app-store rejection delaying a launch and its payment, OS-version
fragmentation requiring open-ended maintenance, and cross-platform
framework limitations forcing an expensive late-stage rewrite to native
code.

## Competitive Advantages

A proven first-attempt app-store approval track record, a dedicated
release manager who owns reviewer communication, and real-device-lab
testing that catches fragmentation bugs a simulator-only workflow misses.

## Launch Strategy

Set up developer accounts and a device-testing lab, build a
pre-submission compliance checklist from known rejection reasons, then
sell the first app through a technical scoping call.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish developer-account setup, the device lab, and the
compliance checklist. Weeks 5-9: scope, quote, and begin building the
first app. Weeks 10-13: submit the first app for review, resolve any
resubmission cycle, and offer the maintenance retainer proactively at
launch.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by adding a second cross-platform engineering pod for concurrent
builds, then specialize into one vertical (field-service, healthtech)
where device-integration expertise commands premium pricing.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client book, active OS-compatibility maintenance retainers,
and app-store developer-account credentials are attractive to a larger
studio looking to acquire a proven mobile delivery team.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for crash-log triage and code review — implementation
accelerators — not for the platform-decision judgment or client
scoping conversation, which stay human-led.
