# Business Blueprint — Origin Route Coffee Co.

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run a specialty-coffee subscription box as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Origin Route Coffee Co. is a curated specialty-coffee subscription box
that ships a rotating single-origin roast from a different independent
roaster each month, with tasting notes and brewing guidance, to home
coffee enthusiasts who want to discover new roasters without doing the
research themselves — a D2C recurring product, distinct from the
physical dine-in coffee-shop business elsewhere in this library.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theConnector` archetype, with at
least two years of relevant experience — a genuine coffee enthusiast
with real palate expertise and the relationship-building instinct to
source rotating roaster partnerships.

## Business Overview

A subscription business that sources roasted beans wholesale from a
rotating roster of independent roaster partners each month, then packs
and ships curated boxes directly to subscribers, with no storefront or
in-house roasting.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from home coffee enthusiasts who want to discover new
roasters and origins. The competitive set includes generic e-commerce
coffee retailers (no curation, no discovery angle) and larger
algorithm-driven subscription boxes (less authentic, less
relationship-driven sourcing).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: home coffee enthusiasts aged 25-45 who
value discovery and quality over convenience-store coffee, plus a
distinct seasonal segment of gift-givers around the winter holidays.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a monthly (or prepaid gift) subscription shipping one
rotating single-origin roast with tasting notes and brewing guidance,
available in single-bag and two-bag tiers.

## Revenue Architecture

Monthly subscription billing generates predictable recurring cash
flow, though inventory prepay and the coffee's freshness window mean
cash is tied up ahead of each month's shipment.

## Pricing Strategy

Monthly subscription tiers: single-bag (€19/month) and two-bag
(€34/month), plus prepaid 3/6/12-month gift subscriptions at a
discount; beans are sourced at wholesale from rotating roaster
partners.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "a new origin in your mailbox, every month — coffee
curated by people who've actually cupped it, not an algorithm."
Primary channel is Instagram and coffee-community content marketing;
roaster co-marketing partnerships are secondary; seasonal
gift-subscription campaigns are experimental.

## Sales System

A discounted first box or free trial converts new visitors quickly —
this is a fast, low-consideration D2C purchase decision, with retention
being the harder ongoing work, not initial conversion.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: monthly roaster-partner selection and bean
sourcing, box assembly and fulfillment, subscriber billing and plan
management, and tasting-note/brewing-guide content production for each
box.

## Technology Stack

Built on Cratejoy as the subscription-commerce platform, ShipStation
for fulfillment and shipping management, and Klaviyo as an optional
tool for email marketing automation.

## Automation Opportunities

Billing, renewal, and shipping-label generation are automated through
the subscription platform; roaster vetting and monthly curation
decisions remain deliberately human.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch, with part-time packing help typically
needed as subscriber volume grows beyond what one person can pack and
ship on the monthly fulfillment date.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €15,000-€28,000 covers subscription-platform
setup, packaging, and an initial inventory prepay — a Medium budget
tier; target monthly income ranges €6,000-€18,000 with an estimated
10-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against monthly subscriber churn, renewal-driven customer
retention, gross margin against wholesale bean and shipping costs, and
monthly recurring revenue growth.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with high AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are the limited freshness window on specialty coffee constraining
inventory planning, dependence on a single roaster partner per month,
and the well-documented industry churn pattern as initial novelty
fades.

## Competitive Advantages

Genuine human taste-testing and curated roaster relationships are hard
for a larger, algorithm-driven competitor to replicate authentically,
subscription revenue provides predictable recurring cash flow, and
rotating partnerships keep the box novel without an in-house roasting
facility.

## Launch Strategy

Set up the subscription-commerce platform and sign the first 2-3
founding roaster partners, then ship a small pilot batch to enthusiast
testers before opening public subscriptions.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: set up the platform, finalize packaging, and sign founding
roaster partners. Weeks 5-8: ship a pilot batch and refine curation
and packaging. Weeks 9-13: launch publicly and ship the first month of
paid subscriber boxes.

## Scaling Strategy

Grow the subscriber base within the core specialty-coffee niche first,
then add a complementary product line (tea, brewing accessories) to
increase order value, then pursue a corporate-gifting wholesale channel
once fulfillment is proven at scale.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented roaster-partner network, subscriber base, and
recurring-revenue history make this sellable to a larger specialty-food
subscription or e-commerce company.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for churn-prediction retention outreach — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping roaster vetting and monthly
curation decisions fully human-led.
