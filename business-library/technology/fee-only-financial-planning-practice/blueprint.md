# Business Blueprint — Clearsum Financial Planning

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run a flat-fee, advice-only financial planning
practice as a business — it is not investment, tax, or financial
advice for any end client, and must never be treated as a substitute
for advice from a licensed professional. Each `##` heading corresponds
to one of the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical
structure._

## Executive Summary

Clearsum Financial Planning is a fully remote, flat-fee financial
planning practice built on a one-time comprehensive plan or an annual
retainer, with no assets-under-management fee and no requirement to
move investment accounts to the advisor — deliberately distinct from
the AUM-fee, hybrid, referral-driven wealth-management practice
elsewhere in this library.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theBuilder` archetype, with at
least four years of relevant experience and CFP certification — a
rigorous, fiduciary-minded specialist comfortable building a fully
remote practice around content and direct client relationships.

## Business Overview

A practice that delivers a comprehensive financial plan and ongoing
advice without managing client investment assets directly, sourced
through content and SEO rather than a professional-referral network.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from younger, high-earning professionals who want
objective planning advice without asset custody. The competitive set
includes AUM-fee wealth managers (require asset transfer, less
accessible to those without significant assets yet) and free
robo-advisors (no personalized comprehensive planning).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: high-earning professionals aged 28-45
who want a comprehensive financial plan without moving their
investment accounts, and who are actively searching online for
planning guidance.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a one-time comprehensive financial plan or an
ongoing annual flat-fee retainer covering plan updates and ad hoc
advice.

## Revenue Architecture

The one-time plan fee is collected relatively quickly after
engagement, but reaching income comparable to AUM-based advisors
requires a larger client base since there's no percentage-of-assets
revenue.

## Pricing Strategy

A one-time comprehensive financial plan (€1,800-€3,500) or an ongoing
annual flat-fee retainer (€2,400-€6,000/year) — no assets-under-
management fee.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "objective advice, no assets required." Primary channel
is long-form SEO financial-education content; niche professional
community engagement is secondary; podcast guest appearances are
experimental.

## Sales System

A prospect discovers content, books a free intro call, and signs a
comprehensive plan or annual retainer — a roughly 21-day cycle while
the prospect gathers documents and evaluates the flat-fee model.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: client intake and goal-setting, plan
drafting and review, ongoing plan monitoring, and compliance
recordkeeping.

## Technology Stack

Built on RightCapital for financial-planning software, with Calendly
and Wealthbox as optional scheduling and CRM tools.

## Automation Opportunities

Scheduling, intake questionnaires, and plan-update reminders are
automated; plan drafting and advice remain fully human-led given the
licensed fiduciary judgment required.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch — capacity is gated by planner hours
until a second CFP-certified planner is added.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €10,000-€25,000 covers CFP certification,
compliance insurance, and planning software — a Medium budget tier,
lower than an AUM-based practice; target monthly income ranges
€5,000-€16,000 with an estimated 11-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against intro-call-to-engagement lead conversion,
plan-to-retainer customer retention, gross margin against software and
compliance costs, and net margin given the low overhead of a fully
remote practice.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty with high AI-resistance — the three biggest risks are
fiduciary-registration and compliance liability, the higher client
volume the flat-fee model requires versus AUM-based advisors, and
client unfamiliarity with the flat-fee model requiring real education.

## Competitive Advantages

The flat-fee, no-AUM model removes the conflict-of-interest concern
many prospects have with AUM-based advisors, fully remote delivery
removes geographic client-acquisition limits, and content-driven
inbound lead generation compounds over time.

## Launch Strategy

Complete CFP certification and fiduciary registration and set up
compliance infrastructure, then validate the flat-fee pricing model
with a first real client engagement priced at the intended standard
rate.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-5: complete certification and compliance setup. Weeks 6-10:
sign and complete the first comprehensive-plan engagement. Weeks
11-13: publish first educational content and pursue the first
retainer conversion.

## Scaling Strategy

Build a compounding content and SEO library, then bring on a second
CFP-certified planner once client volume exceeds solo capacity, then
add a lower-touch group offering for prospects not yet ready for full
engagement.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client base, retainer contracts, and compliance history
make this sellable to a larger fee-only advisory network or
registered investment advisor firm.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for plan-draft summarization assistance — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping plan drafting and advice
fully human-led.
