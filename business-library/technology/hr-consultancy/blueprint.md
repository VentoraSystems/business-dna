# Business Blueprint — Northbridge HR Partners

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a fractional HR consultancy as a business — it is not
employment-law compliance or legal advice for any end client, and should
never be used as a substitute for advice from a licensed professional given
to a specific person or business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of
the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Northbridge HR Partners is a fractional HR consultancy serving small
businesses that need HR expertise without a full-time hire. The business
snapshot: an SHRM-certified consultant runs an HR audit, drafts policies
and handbooks, and grows toward a base of standing monthly retainer
clients. It's for a people-centered operator calm under sensitive
employee-relations situations, not someone seeking fast transactional
sales.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theOperator` or `theConnector` archetype — someone who
naturally becomes the trusted outside voice on hard people decisions.
Five-plus years of prior HR experience meaningfully shortens the
credibility gap with prospects.

## Business Overview

A fractional-HR consultancy positioned on cost-effective expertise for
small businesses that have outgrown ad-hoc HR handling.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for fractional HR support is rising faster than demand for
full-time HR hires among small businesses; the competitive landscape
includes generalist business consultants and full HR outsourcing firms,
with independents winning on specialization and responsiveness.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are small businesses (10-75 employees) with no
dedicated HR function. Their core pain point is having no documented HR
policies, leaving them exposed on compliance basics.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is an HR audit followed by a monthly fractional-HR
retainer covering policy, employee-relations support, and quarterly
compliance check-ins.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue blends a slower-building retainer base with faster, less
predictable project-based fees for handbook and policy work.

## Pricing Strategy

Monthly fractional-HR retainer or a project-based fee for defined
deliverables such as handbook creation.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "the HR department you don't have to hire full-time."
Primary channel is referrals from accountants and business consultants;
practical HR content builds secondary awareness.

## Sales System

A consultative, needs-assessment-led sale — an HR audit is typically the
first step, with a roughly 45-day sales cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: HR audit and needs assessment, policy and
handbook drafting, ongoing employee-relations support, and quarterly
compliance check-ins.

## Technology Stack

Built on BambooHR as a recommended HRIS for clients, Gusto for
payroll/benefits questions, and Lattice for performance-management
guidance.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted policy drafting and job-description generation speed up
first drafts, always followed by full human review before delivery.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; an associate HR consultant is typically
the first hire once the retainer base is stable.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €8,000-€25,000 covers certification, HRIS
software, and professional liability insurance; target monthly income
ranges €5,000-€18,000 with an estimated 9-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against monthly recurring retainer revenue, client retention,
HR-audit-to-retainer conversion rate, and gross margin.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and high AI-resistance. The two biggest risks are
mishandling a confidential employee-relations matter and HR spend being
one of the first line items cut in a downturn.

## Competitive Advantages

SHRM-CP certification signals credibility, fast turnaround on urgent
employee-relations issues builds trust, and the fractional model costs
a fraction of a full-time HR hire.

## Launch Strategy

Get certified and insured first, then activate the referral network of
accountants and business consultants — the highest-trust introduction
source for this audience.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish certification and insurance setup. Weeks 5-8: complete
HR audits for the first 3-5 clients with a clearly defined scope. Weeks
9-13: standardize the audit-and-handbook workflow and publish tiered
retainer pricing.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by moving project-based clients onto standing retainers, then
hiring an associate HR consultant who shares the same judgment on
sensitive matters.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented retainer client book with logged case history is
attractive to larger HR consultancies looking to acquire a book of
recurring clients.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for first-draft policy and job-description work, not
for judgment calls on sensitive employee-relations situations.
