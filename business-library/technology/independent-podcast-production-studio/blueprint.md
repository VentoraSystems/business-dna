# Business Blueprint — Tapehouse Audio

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an independent podcast production studio as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Tapehouse Audio is a full-service podcast production studio handling
recording coordination, editing, mixing, show notes, and distribution
for founder-led thought-leadership podcasts and brand-sponsored
podcast series, monetized through a show-launch package converting
into an ongoing per-episode production retainer.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theOperator` archetype, with at
least two years of relevant experience — a craft-driven audio
specialist with the operational reliability to deliver a consistent
weekly episode schedule.

## Business Overview

A studio that handles the full technical and editorial workflow of a
client's podcast — recording coordination, editing, show notes, and
distribution — so the client only shows up to record.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from founders and executives who want a professional-
sounding thought-leadership podcast without learning audio production
themselves. The competitive set includes DIY editing (cheaper, lower
quality, time-consuming) and large agencies (higher cost, less
personal service).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: founders and executives launching a
personal-brand podcast, and brands sponsoring a branded podcast series,
who value professional production quality and consistent delivery.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a one-time show-launch package (concept, branding,
distribution setup) that converts into an ongoing per-episode
production retainer.

## Revenue Architecture

The launch package is paid upfront and retainers bill monthly in
advance, so cash arrives quickly relative to longer project-based
engagements.

## Pricing Strategy

A one-time show-launch package (€800-€2,000) plus an ongoing
per-episode production retainer (€400-€900 per episode, typically 2-4
episodes per month).

## Marketing System

Positioning: "we make the mic disappear, so the story doesn't."
Primary channel is referrals from impressed podcast guests; LinkedIn
thought-leadership content for founders is secondary; podcast industry
directory listings are experimental.

## Sales System

A founder or brand books a discovery call, the studio scopes format
and cadence, then sells the launch package converting to retainer — a
roughly 14-day cycle, often closed quickly once a demo reel is heard.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: show concept and format design, recording
coordination, editing/mixing/mastering, and show-notes/distribution.

## Technology Stack

Built on Descript for AI-assisted editing and transcription,
Riverside.fm for remote recording, and Transistor as an optional
podcast hosting tool.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted transcription and rough-cut editing speed up initial
processing; final mixing, pacing, and quality control remain
deliberately hands-on.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch — capacity is gated by editing hours
per concurrent show until a second editor is added.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €4,000-€10,000 covers audio equipment and
editing software — a Low budget tier; target monthly income ranges
€4,000-€12,000 with an estimated 6-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against show-launch close rate, client-show retention, gross
margin against editing-time costs, and churn given the industry's
novelty-fade pattern.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with low AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are AI editing tools commoditizing manual work, client shows being
abandoned after initial novelty fades, and a hard capacity ceiling per
editor.

## Competitive Advantages

A polished, professional sound is a strong personal-brand
differentiator, referrals from impressed guests create a low-cost
acquisition channel, and a documented workflow template shortens
onboarding for new client shows.

## Launch Strategy

Build a strong demo reel with modest equipment, then validate the
pricing model with a first real client priced at the intended standard
rate before broader outreach.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: set up equipment and build a demo reel. Weeks 5-9: sign and
launch the first client show. Weeks 10-13: deliver the first few
episodes on a consistent schedule and begin referral outreach.

## Scaling Strategy

Document a production-workflow template, then hire a second editor
once concurrent show capacity is maxed, then add an executive-producer
tier for larger brand clients.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client roster, production workflow, and recurring-revenue
history make this sellable to a larger media-production or agency
company.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for transcription and rough-cut editing — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping final mixing and quality
control fully human-led.
