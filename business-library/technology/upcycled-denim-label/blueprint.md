# Business Blueprint — Reweave Denim Co.

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an upcycled denim fashion label as a business.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Reweave Denim Co. is a small-batch fashion label that sources
secondhand denim and factory deadstock, hand-transforms it into
limited-run garments, and sells primarily D2C through scarcity-driven
drop releases, with secondary wholesale and made-to-order revenue —
deliberately capacity-constrained by hand production, trading scale
for authenticity and premium pricing.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theVisionary` archetype, with at
least two years of relevant experience — a craft-driven specialist
with real sewing skill and a design vision strong enough to turn
unpredictable secondhand materials into a coherent collection.

## Business Overview

A label that sources and hand-transforms secondhand denim into
finished garments, sold primarily direct-to-consumer through
scarcity-driven limited-run drops, with secondary wholesale and
custom-commission revenue.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from eco-conscious fashion consumers seeking authentic,
one-of-a-kind pieces. The competitive set includes mass-market
"sustainable" fast fashion (higher volume, less authentic) and
independent upcyclers on marketplaces like Depop (less brand
cohesion, no consistent drop cadence).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2C: eco-conscious consumers aged 20-35
willing to pay a premium for one-of-a-kind sustainable pieces, active
in visual social platforms and sustainable-fashion communities.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a limited-run drop of 15-40 unique garments,
supported by secondary wholesale orders to boutiques and occasional
made-to-order custom commissions.

## Revenue Architecture

Drop releases generate concentrated bursts of revenue around each
launch; the time between drops, driven by production capacity and
material sourcing, keeps overall revenue speed moderate.

## Pricing Strategy

Limited-run drops priced €120-€280 per garment; wholesale to boutique
retailers at roughly 50% off retail; occasional made-to-order custom
commissions at a premium.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "every pair has already lived a life — we give it a
second one." Primary channel is before/after transformation content on
visual platforms; sustainable-fashion marketplaces and pop-ups are
secondary; boutique wholesale partnerships are experimental.

## Sales System

A drop model releases 15-40 pieces at a time to a notified email/SMS
list — a fast, scarcity-driven, roughly 1-day conversion cycle once a
customer is on the list.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: deadstock sourcing and grading, garment
deconstruction and reconstruction, drop production and quality
control, and D2C/wholesale fulfillment.

## Technology Stack

Built on Shopify for the e-commerce storefront, Klaviyo for email/SMS
drop notifications, and CLO 3D as an optional pattern-design tool.

## Automation Opportunities

Drop notifications and checkout are automated; sourcing, construction,
and design remain deliberately hands-on, since craftsmanship is the
actual product.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch — capacity is gated by the founder's
own hand-production hours until a part-time production assistant is
added.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €12,000-€28,000 covers production equipment,
initial deadstock sourcing, and an e-commerce storefront — a Medium
budget tier; target monthly income ranges €4,500-€14,000 with an
estimated 9-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against notification-list-to-purchase conversion, repeat-drop
customer retention, gross margin against material and labor costs, and
wholesale close rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with high AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are unpredictable finite deadstock supply, hand-production capacity
limits, and greenwashing skepticism requiring genuinely verifiable
sourcing claims.

## Competitive Advantages

An authentic upcycling story and small-batch scarcity create a
defensible differentiation mass-market fast fashion can't replicate,
before/after content is native and highly shareable, and multiple
revenue channels diversify income.

## Launch Strategy

Set up the production workshop and source initial deadstock, then
produce and sell a small pilot drop at the intended standard price to
validate design direction and pricing before a full public launch.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-5: set up the workshop and source initial materials. Weeks
6-9: produce and sell a pilot drop. Weeks 10-13: launch publicly with
the first full drop and begin transformation content marketing.

## Scaling Strategy

Build a reliable multi-supplier deadstock network, then bring on a
part-time production assistant to increase drop volume, then expand
into wholesale partnerships with sustainable-fashion boutiques.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented supplier network, brand IP, and sales history make this
sellable to a larger sustainable-fashion or lifestyle brand.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for social-content caption assistance — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping sourcing, construction, and
design fully human-led.
