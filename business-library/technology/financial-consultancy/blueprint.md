# Business Blueprint — Beacon Wealth Advisory

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a fee-only financial planning practice as a business — it is
not investment, tax, or financial advice for any end client, and should never
be used as a substitute for advice from a licensed professional given to a
specific person. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Beacon Wealth Advisory is a fee-only, fiduciary financial planning
practice serving pre-retirees and business owners planning an exit. The
business snapshot: a CFP-certified advisor builds long-term financial
plans and manages assets under a fee-only model, with revenue growing
slowly through trust-based referrals rather than paid acquisition. It's
for a patient, relationship-first operator, not someone chasing fast
transactional sales.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theConnector` or `theSpecialist` archetype — someone
who builds long-term trust naturally and is disciplined enough to earn
and maintain a fiduciary certification. At least 5 years of prior
financial-services experience meaningfully shortens the credibility gap
with prospects.

## Business Overview

A fee-only wealth advisory practice built on assets-under-management
fees and flat financial-planning fees, positioned against
commission-based competitors on fiduciary trust.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for fee-only, conflict-free advice is rising as awareness of
commission-based conflicts of interest grows; the competitive landscape
includes both large wirehouse advisors and independent fee-only
practices, with independents winning on trust and personalization.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are pre-retirees aged 45-65 with meaningful
investable assets, and business owners planning an exit. Their core
pain point is uncertainty about whether current savings support their
desired retirement lifestyle.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a comprehensive financial plan, delivered for a flat
fee or ongoing AUM-based fee, with quarterly review meetings included.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is AUM-based and compounds slowly as assets under management
and referrals grow — a genuinely slow ramp rather than a fast one.

## Pricing Strategy

Value-based pricing: either a percentage of assets under management, or
a flat fee for a standalone financial plan.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "financial plans built for decades, not quarters."
Primary channel is referrals from accountants and estate attorneys;
small educational seminars build secondary trust.

## Sales System

A long, trust-building relationship sale — a complimentary discovery
meeting is the standard first step, with a roughly 90-day sales cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: initial discovery and goals assessment, plan
drafting and presentation, quarterly portfolio reviews, and an annual
comprehensive plan refresh.

## Technology Stack

Built on RightCapital for financial-plan drafting, Riskalyze for
risk-tolerance assessment, and Redtail CRM for the long-term client
relationship record.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted meeting-note summarization speeds up post-meeting
documentation without touching the advice itself, which stays entirely
human-led.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; a junior advisor under a fee-split
arrangement is typically the first hire once assets under management
cross a meaningful threshold.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €15,000-€50,000 covers CFP certification,
compliance setup, and planning software; target monthly income ranges
€5,000-€25,000 with an estimated 14-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against annual recurring revenue, client-acquisition cost,
client lifetime value, and client retention.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty and high AI-resistance. The two biggest risks are
strict, jurisdiction-varying regulatory licensing requirements and
AUM-fee sensitivity to market downturns.

## Competitive Advantages

CFP certification signals fiduciary rigor; the fee-only structure
removes the commission conflict-of-interest objection entirely; a
personalized quarterly-review cadence keeps retention high.

## Launch Strategy

Get CFP-certified and licensed first, then activate the professional
referral network — accountants and estate attorneys are the highest-
trust introduction sources, not cold advertising.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish CFP certification and regulatory licensing. Weeks
5-8: complete financial plans for the first 3-5 clients, even at a
reduced rate, to build a documented track record. Weeks 9-13:
standardize the plan-drafting workflow and host the first small
educational seminar.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by deepening the centers-of-influence referral network across
multiple professional verticals, then adding junior advisors under a
fee-split arrangement once assets under management justify it.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented AUM book with complete CRM records is attractive to
larger advisory firms looking to acquire a book of long-term client
relationships.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for meeting documentation and portfolio-risk
flagging, not for generating investment advice — the fiduciary
relationship stays human-led.
