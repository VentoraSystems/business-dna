# Business Blueprint — Harborlight Insurance Brokerage

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow an independent insurance brokerage as a business — it is not
insurance, coverage, or claims advice for any end client, and should never be
used as a substitute for advice from a licensed insurance professional given
to a specific person or business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the
25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Harborlight Insurance Brokerage is an independent, multi-carrier
insurance brokerage serving families and small-business owners. The
business snapshot: a licensed broker compares coverage across 4-5+
carriers per line of business, earns commission on new business and
policy renewals, and grows the book primarily through referrals from
real-estate agents and lenders. It's for a natural relationship-builder
who enjoys sales conversations, not a purely technical operator.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theConnector` or `theHustler` archetype — someone who
builds rapport quickly and is comfortable with a consultative sales
process. Two-plus years of prior insurance or sales experience
meaningfully shortens the ramp-up to a sustainable client book.

## Business Overview

An independent, multi-carrier brokerage positioned against captive
single-insurer agents on choice, comparison shopping, and hands-on
claims advocacy.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for independent, comparison-shopping brokers remains steady as
consumers increasingly expect price transparency; the competitive
landscape includes captive agents (tied to one carrier) and other
independents, with differentiation coming from claims-support quality.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are homeowners, families, and small-business owners
(1-50 employees) needing personal or commercial coverage. Their core
pain point is feeling locked into one insurer's pricing with no easy
way to compare.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a free, multi-carrier comparative coverage quote,
followed by policy binding and ongoing renewal re-shopping and claims
advocacy.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue is commission-based: new-business commission plus renewal
commission, which compounds into a recurring base as the client book
grows.

## Pricing Strategy

Commission-based pricing paid by the carrier — no direct fee to the
client for most personal-lines policies; commercial-lines consulting
may carry a separate fee.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "the right coverage, from someone who actually shops it
for you." Primary channel is referrals from existing clients,
real-estate agents, and lenders; local event sponsorship builds
secondary awareness.

## Sales System

A consultative needs-analysis sale comparing quotes across multiple
carriers, with a roughly 21-day sales cycle for personal lines.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: needs analysis and comparative quoting,
policy binding, annual renewal review and re-shopping, and claims
support advocacy.

## Technology Stack

Built on EZLynx for comparative rating and Applied Epic or HawkSoft as
the agency management system for policy and renewal tracking.

## Automation Opportunities

Automated comparative rating and renewal-reminder sequences meaningfully
reduce missed renewal windows and speed up quote turnaround, without
touching the actual coverage recommendation.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; a second producer under a
commission-split arrangement is typically the first hire once the
commercial-lines book justifies it.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €10,000-€30,000 covers licensing, carrier
appointments, E&O insurance, and an agency management system; target
monthly income ranges €5,000-€20,000 with an estimated 10-month path to
break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against annual renewal commission revenue, client retention,
quote close rate, and referral-lead conversion.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and moderate AI-resistance. The two biggest risks
are losing a key carrier appointment and errors & omissions liability
exposure.

## Competitive Advantages

Multi-carrier comparison shopping (not captive to one insurer), CIC
certification for commercial-lines credibility, and fast, hands-on
claims advocacy.

## Launch Strategy

Get licensed and carrier-appointed first, then activate the referral
network of real-estate agents and lenders — the highest-conversion
introduction sources, not cold advertising.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish licensing and secure carrier appointments. Weeks 5-8:
bind the first 5-10 personal-lines policies, always presenting at
least 3 comparative quotes. Weeks 9-13: standardize the needs-analysis
workflow and announce the brokerage to the referral network.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by cross-selling additional policies to the existing client book,
then building a dedicated commercial-lines practice for higher-premium
accounts once the personal-lines base is stable.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented book of business with complete AMS records is
attractive to larger agencies looking to acquire a book of renewal
relationships.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for comparative rating and renewal reminders, not
for coverage recommendations — the actual advice stays human-led and
documented.
