# AI Notes — Latitude HR Advisory

_Mirrors `features/business-dna`'s `AiMetadata` (`business-dna.json`'s
`aiMetadata` field) in plain prose. Educational business-building notes
only — not employment-law compliance or legal advice for an end client,
and the multi-jurisdiction scope of this practice raises the compliance
stakes further than a single-jurisdiction generalist HR practice._

## Matching Hints

Best matched to someone strong in management and finance skills, with
detail-oriented and analytical personality traits. Requires a
€12,000-€28,000 budget, moderate risk tolerance, 30-45 hours/week
availability, remote work preference, and no travel requirement. Ideal
founder archetypes: theSpecialist, theOperator.

## Generation Hints

Tone should be precise and credibly expert on compliance mechanics —
this audience is sophisticated (often VC-backed operators) and will
discount vague reassurance; name the specific mechanics (statutory
benefits, notice periods, EOR-vs-entity trade-offs).

## Blueprint Hints

Lead the Executive Summary with the multi-jurisdiction complexity
problem — venture-backed remote-first companies feel this pain acutely
the moment they hire their second country, unlike single-jurisdiction
small businesses.

## Marketing Hints

EOR-platform co-marketing and remote-first startup community presence
convert far better than generic HR-service content for this audience.

## Financial Hints

Model the retainer as scaling with both headcount and jurisdiction
count — a client adding a third hiring country is a materially
different, higher-value retainer tier than one adding headcount within
an existing jurisdiction.
