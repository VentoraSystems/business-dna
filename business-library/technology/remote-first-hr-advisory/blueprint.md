# Business Blueprint — Latitude HR Advisory

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a multi-jurisdiction HR advisory as a business — it is not
employment-law compliance or legal advice for any end client, and should
never be used as a substitute for advice from a licensed professional given
to a specific person or business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of
the 25 sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Latitude HR Advisory is an HR advisory built specifically for fully-
distributed, venture-backed companies — not local small businesses. The
business snapshot: a GPHR-certified specialist runs jurisdiction-
expansion audits, advises on Employer-of-Record versus direct-entity
hiring, reviews contractor-misclassification risk, and grows toward a
base of standing multi-jurisdiction retainer clients tiered by headcount
and hiring-jurisdiction count. It's for a compliance-minded specialist
with genuine multi-jurisdiction fluency and the process discipline of an
operator, not a generalist HR consultant improvising country by country.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theOperator` archetype — someone who
combines verified employment-law depth with the operational rigor to
run repeatable jurisdiction-review cycles across many clients at once.
Six-plus years of prior HR or global-mobility experience meaningfully
shortens the credibility gap with sophisticated, VC-backed prospects.

## Business Overview

A multi-jurisdiction HR advisory positioned on the specific complexity
of hiring across states and countries at once, for remote-first
companies that have outgrown single-jurisdiction HR handling.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for multi-jurisdiction compliance guidance is rising fastest
among venture-backed remote-first companies past their first
international hire; the competitive landscape includes generalist HR
consultancies stretching into cross-border work and the EOR platforms
themselves, with independents winning on advisory depth the platforms'
support teams can't provide.

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are venture-backed, remote-first startups and
scale-ups (roughly 20-200 employees) hiring across multiple US states
and/or countries at once — not local SMBs with a single-jurisdiction
workforce. Their core pain point is not knowing what changes legally,
in statutory benefits and termination rules, each time they hire into
a new jurisdiction.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a jurisdiction-expansion audit followed by a monthly
multi-jurisdiction retainer covering EOR-vs-entity guidance,
misclassification risk review, ongoing regulatory monitoring, and
remote-operations framework design.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue blends a steadily-growing retainer base — scaling with both
client headcount and hiring-jurisdiction count — with faster, higher-
margin jurisdiction-expansion audit fees triggered by a specific
upcoming hire.

## Pricing Strategy

Monthly retainer tiered by headcount and number of hiring jurisdictions,
plus one-off project fees for jurisdiction-expansion audits such as
"we're hiring our first employee in Germany — audit what changes."

## Marketing System

Positioning: "HR and employment-compliance guidance built for companies
that hire everywhere at once — not a generalist HR consultancy with a
remote label." Primary channels are EOR-platform co-marketing and
remote-first startup community engagement; VC portfolio-company
introductions and jurisdiction-specific content build secondary
awareness.

## Sales System

A consultative, urgency-led sale — a scoped jurisdiction-expansion
audit, triggered by a specific upcoming hire, is typically the first
paid engagement, with a roughly 35-day sales cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: jurisdiction-expansion audit and readiness
review, multi-jurisdiction employment-law monitoring and playbook
maintenance, EOR-vs-direct-entity structuring guidance, worker and
contractor misclassification risk review, and remote-culture and
operations framework design (async norms, time-zone-fair meetings and
PTO, distributed onboarding).

## Technology Stack

Built on Deel and Remote as recommended EOR platforms for clients
hiring without a local entity, and Rippling as a recommended global
HRIS for clients managing HR and payroll across multiple entities.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted jurisdiction-compliance research summarization and policy
localization speed up first drafts across many jurisdictions at once,
always followed by verification against a primary legal source before
delivery.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; a regional specialist consultant
owning a jurisdiction cluster (e.g. the EU) is typically the first hire
once the retainer base is stable.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €12,000-€28,000 covers a compliance-database
subscription, EOR-platform partner accreditation, and cross-border
professional liability insurance; target monthly income ranges
€9,000-€26,000 with an estimated 10-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against monthly recurring retainer revenue, multi-jurisdiction
retainer retention, audit-to-retainer conversion rate, and gross margin.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty and high AI-resistance. The three biggest risks are the
independent, fast-changing pace of employment law across every active
jurisdiction, the outsized liability of a wrong EOR-vs-entity or
classification call, and channel concentration risk from relying too
heavily on a small number of EOR-platform partners.

## Competitive Advantages

Deep multi-jurisdiction compliance fluency is hard for a generalist HR
consultant to replicate quickly, EOR-platform partnerships put the
advisory in front of companies exactly when the compliance question
becomes urgent, and each jurisdiction playbook becomes progressively
cheaper to reuse across clients.

## Launch Strategy

Verify jurisdiction expertise and get insured first, then activate
EOR-platform co-marketing and remote-first startup communities — the
highest-intent introduction sources for this audience.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: verify jurisdiction playbooks and finish insurance setup.
Weeks 5-8: complete jurisdiction-expansion audits for the first 3-5
clients with a standardized audit checklist. Weeks 9-13: standardize
the audit-and-playbook workflow and publish the tiered headcount-times-
jurisdiction retainer pricing.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by converting jurisdiction-expansion audit clients onto standing
multi-jurisdiction retainers, then hiring regional specialist
consultants who each own a jurisdiction cluster and are co-reviewed for
a full quarter before advising clients independently.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented multi-jurisdiction retainer client book with reusable
jurisdiction playbooks is attractive to larger global-employment
advisories or EOR platforms looking to acquire an advisory layer.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for first-pass jurisdiction research and policy
localization, never for the final judgment call on an EOR-vs-entity or
classification recommendation.
