# Business Blueprint — Lucidwell Data Partners

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how
to build and grow a solo-deliverable data analytics consultancy as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Lucidwell Data Partners is a solo-deliverable data analytics consultancy:
a single data consultant builds ETL pipelines, BI dashboards, and
predictive models for mid-market companies with disconnected data (scattered
spreadsheets, siloed tools) and no dedicated data team. Unlike
`ai-consulting`, a different business in this same `technology-services`
category, this business actually builds things rather than delivering
pure strategic advice. It's for someone who can both untangle a genuinely
messy pipeline and explain the result in one sentence a non-technical
executive needs to hear.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theBuilder` or `theSpecialist` archetype, with at least
four years of data-engineering or analytics experience. The strongest
`analyst` archetype match of any business in this category — this work
rewards genuine analytical rigor more than sales charisma.

## Business Overview

A fixed-scope data pipeline and dashboard consultancy serving mid-market
companies whose data lives in disconnected spreadsheets and tools, with
no dedicated in-house data team to unify it.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from operations and finance leaders drowning in manual
spreadsheet reporting who can't yet justify a full in-house data hire.
The competitive set includes generalist software houses (weaker on data
modeling specifically) and increasingly capable no-code BI tools
(cheaper, but poor at messy multi-source integration and predictive
modeling).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: operations and finance leaders at
mid-market companies. Common pain points are a leadership team that keeps
asking the same question no current spreadsheet can answer, and data
scattered across tools that were never designed to talk to each other.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a fixed-scope data pipeline and dashboard build,
always starting with a free data-audit call, followed by an optional
monthly analytics retainer covering maintenance and ongoing insights
reporting.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue starts as lumpy, fully bespoke project sales; as a reusable
template library grows, later projects close faster and a genuinely
recurring analytics-retainer layer compounds alongside them — the
fastest-improving margin trajectory of any business in this category.

## Pricing Strategy

Fixed-scope pricing set after a free data-audit call reveals the real
state of the client's data sources — this protects margin from the
single biggest risk in this business: undiscovered data-quality issues.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "turn your spreadsheets into one dashboard that actually
answers the question." Primary channel is published before/after
dashboard-transformation case studies; founder-network referrals and
targeted outbound build secondary and experimental awareness.

## Sales System

A free data-audit call reviews the client's existing sources and the
specific business question they can't currently answer, with a roughly
28-day cycle from first conversation to signed project.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: a data-source audit and scoping call, an ETL
pipeline build, dashboard design with stakeholder review cycles, and
monthly data-freshness monitoring for retainer clients.

## Technology Stack

Built on dbt for data transformation, Metabase for dashboards, Fivetran
for data ingestion, and Python/pandas for custom processing — one of the
most automation-forward operating models in this category, since the
business's own delivery process is itself built from reusable pipelines.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted data cleaning accelerates identifying and correcting
data-quality issues; AI drafts a first-pass narrative summary of
dashboard trends for the monthly retainer report, always verified by the
consultant before delivery.

## Team Structure

Highly solo-founder-friendly — a second data consultant is typically the
first hire once a reusable template library exists to onboard them
against.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €9,000-€24,000 covers data-warehouse setup, BI
tooling, and a portfolio site; target monthly income ranges
€13,000-€36,000 with an estimated 7-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against data-audit-to-signed-project close rate, gross margin
(improving as the template library grows), retainer conversion (customer
retention), and analytics-retainer MRR growth.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty and low AI-resistance — of the businesses in this
category, basic dashboard-building work is among the most exposed to
AI-native BI tools and no-code builders. The three biggest risks are
mid-project data-quality surprises, silent predictive-model accuracy
degradation, and commoditization of basic dashboard work.

## Competitive Advantages

A reusable library of pipeline templates and dashboard components that
makes later engagements faster and higher-margin, a free data-audit call
that makes the sales conversation concrete, and an analytics retainer
that keeps dashboards from going stale and unused.

## Launch Strategy

Set up the data warehouse and BI tooling, build 2-3 reusable pipeline
templates and a sample dashboard as a portfolio piece, then sell the
first project through a free data-audit call.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: finish tooling setup and build the first reusable templates.
Weeks 5-9: run data-audit calls and deliver the first pipeline/dashboard
project through iterative stakeholder review. Weeks 10-13: publish the
first before/after case study and offer the analytics retainer
proactively at delivery.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by building a library of reusable pipeline templates and dashboard
components across common data-source combinations, then bring on a
second data consultant to run concurrent engagements against that shared
library.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client book, active analytics retainers, and a standardized
template library are attractive to a larger data or software consultancy
looking to acquire a proven delivery process and recurring-revenue base.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for data cleaning and first-draft insight narratives
— implementation accelerators — while keeping the business-question
translation and stakeholder communication fully human-led.
