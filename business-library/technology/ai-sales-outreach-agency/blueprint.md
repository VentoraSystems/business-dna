# Business Blueprint — Pipeline Forge

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run an AI-powered B2B outbound sales agency as a
business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Pipeline Forge is an AI-powered B2B outbound sales agency that builds
and runs personalized cold-email and LinkedIn campaigns for clients
with a sales team but no dedicated prospecting function, monetized
through a paid pilot campaign converting into a monthly per-campaign
retainer plus an optional per-meeting performance bonus.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theHustler` or `theOperator` archetype, with at least
three years of relevant sales or growth experience — a persistent,
sales-native operator comfortable proving the agency's value through
its own live outbound campaigns.

## Business Overview

An agency that builds, launches, and manages AI-personalized cold-email
and LinkedIn outreach campaigns on behalf of B2B clients, handing off
qualified booked meetings for the client's own sales team to close.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from B2B companies with a sales team but no dedicated
outbound/SDR function. The competitive set includes in-house SDR hires
(more expensive, slower to ramp) and self-serve AI outreach tools
(cheaper, but require in-house expertise to run effectively).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: sales and revenue leaders at 10-100
employee SaaS or services companies who need pipeline but don't want
to build an outbound function from scratch.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a paid pilot campaign (ICP research, list building,
campaign launch) that converts into an ongoing monthly retainer per
active campaign, with an optional performance bonus per booked
meeting.

## Revenue Architecture

Pilot campaigns are paid upfront and retainers bill in advance, so
cash arrives quickly relative to longer, discovery-heavy consulting
engagements.

## Pricing Strategy

A €2,000-€4,000 pilot campaign converts into an ongoing monthly
retainer of €2,500-€6,000 per active campaign, with an optional
€150-€300 performance bonus per qualified booked meeting beyond a
baseline target.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "booked meetings, not just sent emails." Primary channel
is the agency's own outbound campaigns targeting prospects — a live
proof-of-concept; B2B sales community engagement is secondary; pipeline
results case-study content is experimental.

## Sales System

A prospect books a discovery call through the agency's own outbound
campaign, the call defines ICP and messaging, and a paid 4-6 week
pilot converts to retainer if it succeeds — a roughly 14-day cycle
from discovery call to signed pilot.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: ICP definition and list building, sequence
copywriting and campaign setup, deliverability monitoring, and
booked-meeting handoff with performance reporting.

## Technology Stack

Built on Clay for lead enrichment and data orchestration, Instantly for
cold-email sending and warmup, and Apollo as an optional b2b contact
database.

## Automation Opportunities

Sending, follow-up sequencing, and reply-routing are substantially
automated; copywriting strategy and deliverability troubleshooting
remain deliberately hands-on.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch — capacity is gated by campaign-manager
hours until a documented playbook is mature enough to onboard a second
campaign manager.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €5,000-€12,000 covers enrichment, sending,
and warmup infrastructure — a Low budget tier; target monthly income
ranges €6,000-€18,000 with an estimated 7-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against sequence-to-meeting lead conversion, pilot-to-retainer
close rate, retainer customer retention, and gross margin against
software costs.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty with low AI-resistance — the three biggest risks
are deliverability restrictions silently killing campaigns, AI-native
tools increasingly enabling clients to run outbound in-house, and
results depending heavily on the client's own product-market fit.

## Competitive Advantages

The agency's own outbound campaigns are a live, continuous
proof-of-concept, a documented playbook of high-converting templates
shortens ramp time on new campaigns, and performance-based bonus
pricing aligns incentives with client outcomes.

## Launch Strategy

Set up enrichment, sending, and warmup infrastructure, then run the
agency's own outbound campaign to a meaningful sample size before
pitching any prospective client.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: set up infrastructure and launch the agency's own outbound
campaign. Weeks 5-9: sign and run the first paid pilot campaign. Weeks
10-13: hand off booked meetings, gather pilot results, and convert to
a retainer.

## Scaling Strategy

Build a documented playbook of the highest-converting templates and
ICPs, then hire a second campaign manager once demand consistently
exceeds one person's capacity, then productize the pilot methodology
into a lower-touch self-serve tier.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented playbook, signed retainer contracts, and pipeline
performance history make this sellable to a larger sales-tech or
growth-agency company.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for personalized sequence copywriting — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping deliverability
troubleshooting and campaign strategy fully human-led.
