# Business Blueprint — Vantage Point Consulting

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes how to
build and grow a strategy and management consultancy as a business — it is
not strategic, legal, or financial advice for any end client, and should
never be used as a substitute for advice from a qualified professional given
to a specific business. Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25
sections in `features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Vantage Point Consulting is a strategy and management consultancy
serving growing companies stuck on a specific strategic decision. The
business snapshot: a consultant runs a signature diagnostic framework
through project-based engagements, and grows toward a mix of advisory
retainers and a productized, fixed-scope offer. It's for a strategic
visionary who can also translate big-picture thinking into a concrete,
executable plan, not someone who prefers pure execution work.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theVisionary` or `theOperator` archetype — someone who
sees the big strategic picture and can still turn it into a sequenced,
actionable plan. Six-plus years of prior operating or leadership
experience meaningfully shortens the credibility gap with prospects.

## Business Overview

A strategy and management consultancy positioned on a signature,
repeatable diagnostic framework rather than generic strategy-deck
exercises.

## Market Intelligence

Demand for fixed-scope, productized diagnostic offers is rising faster
than demand for fully open-ended strategy engagements; the competitive
landscape includes large strategy firms (expensive, slow) and
generalist consultants (broad but shallow).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customers are growing companies (20-200 employees) facing a
specific strategic decision without an internal strategy function.
Their core pain point is too many internal opinions on what to do next,
with no structured way to decide.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer is a discovery-and-diagnostic engagement producing a
clear, sequenced plan, with a productized fixed-scope version available
for the most common engagement type.

## Revenue Architecture

Revenue blends lumpy project fees with a slower-building, steadier
advisory-retainer base.

## Pricing Strategy

Project-based fee for a defined engagement or a monthly advisory
retainer for ongoing guidance.

## Marketing System

Positioning: "clarity on what to do next, and the plan to do it."
Primary channel is referrals from past clients and complementary
professionals; thought-leadership content and case studies build
secondary awareness.

## Sales System

A consultative, thought-leadership-led sale — prospects often arrive
already familiar with the firm's published frameworks, with a roughly
45-day sales cycle.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: discovery and diagnostic assessment, strategy
and roadmap development, implementation support, and quarterly business
reviews for retainer clients.

## Technology Stack

Built on Miro for workshop facilitation, Notion for deliverable
management, and a CRM such as HubSpot for the client pipeline.

## Automation Opportunities

AI-assisted research synthesis and presentation drafting speed up the
discovery phase, always followed by the consultant's own analysis and
judgment.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch; an associate consultant is typically
the first hire once engagement volume justifies it.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €12,000-€35,000 covers CRM setup, workshop
tools, and case-study portfolio production; target monthly income
ranges €6,000-€25,000 with an estimated 9-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against annual revenue, discovery-call close rate, project-to-
retainer conversion rate, and content-to-call conversion rate.

## Risk Analysis

Moderate difficulty and high AI-resistance. The two biggest risks are
reputation exposure tied to visible client outcomes and lumpy,
unpredictable project-based revenue.

## Competitive Advantages

A signature, repeatable diagnostic framework, a strong case-study
portfolio with measurable outcomes, and a thought-leadership content
pipeline that pre-sells prospects on the firm's approach.

## Launch Strategy

Document 2-3 case studies before launching, then activate the referral
network and publish the first thought-leadership content around the
signature framework.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: set up the CRM and document early case studies. Weeks 5-8:
complete the first 2-3 discovery-to-strategy engagements with a fixed
scope. Weeks 9-13: formalize the diagnostic framework and publish the
first case study.

## Scaling Strategy

Scale by productizing the most common engagement into a fixed-scope,
fixed-price offer to shorten sales cycles, then building a small
associate-consultant bench trained on the documented framework.

## Exit Opportunities

A well-documented framework, client book, and case-study portfolio are
attractive to larger consultancies looking to acquire a specialized
practice.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for research synthesis and presentation drafting,
never as a replacement for the consultant's own strategic judgment.
