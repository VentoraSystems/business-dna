# Business Blueprint — Northgate IP Partners

_Educational business-building guidance only. This document describes
how to build and run a boutique intellectual-property law practice as
a business — it is not legal advice for any end client, and must never
be treated as a substitute for advice from a licensed patent attorney.
Each `##` heading corresponds to one of the 25 sections in
`features/blueprint`'s v2 canonical structure._

## Executive Summary

Northgate IP Partners is a boutique intellectual-property law practice
serving startups and independent creators with trademark registration,
patent prosecution, and ongoing IP-portfolio management, built on
patent-bar-qualified specialization rather than general business-law
practice — deliberately distinct from the general-practice Law Firm
elsewhere in this library.

## Entrepreneur Fit

Best suited to `theSpecialist` or `theVisionary` archetype, with at
least five years of relevant experience and patent-bar qualification —
a specialist with genuine technical fluency in at least one technology
vertical.

## Business Overview

A fully remote boutique practice that drafts, files, and prosecutes
trademark and patent applications for startup and independent-creator
clients, then maintains their IP portfolio under an optional annual
retainer.

## Market Intelligence

Demand comes from startups and independent creators who need
specialized IP counsel beyond what a general-practice law firm offers.
The competitive set includes general-practice law firms (broader but
less deep IP expertise) and large IP boutiques (deeper resources, much
higher rates, less accessible to early-stage clients).

## Customer Intelligence

Primary customer type is B2B: technical founders and independent
inventors who need trademark registration or patent prosecution and
value demonstrated technical credibility over generalist legal
services.

## Offer Architecture

The core offer splits by matter type: flat-fee trademark registration
and hourly-billed patent prosecution, with an optional annual
IP-portfolio monitoring and renewal retainer for ongoing clients.

## Revenue Architecture

Trademark flat fees are collected relatively quickly at filing, but
patent-prosecution revenue depends on unpredictable examiner response
timelines and can take a year or more to fully realize.

## Pricing Strategy

Flat-fee trademark registration (€1,200-€3,000 per mark), hourly-billed
patent prosecution (€250-€400/hour, typically €8,000-€25,000 total to
grant), and an annual IP-portfolio retainer (€2,000-€6,000/year).

## Marketing System

Positioning: "protect the idea, not just the paperwork." Primary
channel is accelerator and VC portfolio referrals; inventor and maker
community content marketing is secondary; technical conference speaking
is experimental.

## Sales System

An intake call assesses patentability or trademark distinctiveness,
followed by a flat-fee (trademark) or hourly (patent) engagement letter
— a roughly 21-day cycle from intake to signed engagement.

## Operations System

Core recurring processes: intake assessment, application drafting and
filing, prosecution and examiner-correspondence management, and
IP-portfolio docketing and renewal-deadline tracking.

## Technology Stack

Built on Anaqua for IP docketing and deadline management, USPTO patent
search databases for prior-art search, and Clio as an optional legal
practice-management tool.

## Automation Opportunities

Docketing and renewal-deadline tracking are automated through
dedicated IP-management software; application drafting and prosecution
strategy remain fully attorney-led.

## Team Structure

Solo-founder-friendly at launch — capacity is gated by patent-bar
qualification until a second qualified associate can be brought on.

## Financial Overview

Startup budget of roughly €35,000-€60,000 covers malpractice insurance,
IP-docketing software, and patent-search database subscriptions — a
High budget tier, above the general-practice Law Firm elsewhere in
this library; target monthly income ranges €9,000-€28,000 with an
estimated 12-month path to break-even.

## KPIs

Tracked against intake-to-engagement close rate, filing-to-retainer
customer retention, gross margin against docketing and database costs,
and net margin given premium specialized billing rates.

## Risk Analysis

High difficulty with high AI-resistance — the three biggest risks are
malpractice liability from a missed deadline or poorly drafted claim,
revenue volatility from unpredictable examiner response timelines, and
ongoing multi-jurisdiction regulatory compliance requirements.

## Competitive Advantages

Patent-bar credentialing creates a genuine expertise moat difficult for
general-practice firms to replicate, deep vertical specialization
builds referral-worthy reputation faster than broad practice, and
annual IP-portfolio retainers provide predictable recurring revenue.

## Launch Strategy

Complete patent-bar qualification and IP-docketing infrastructure
setup, then validate the practice's positioning and pricing with a
first real engagement priced at the intended standard rate.

## 90-Day Action Plan

Weeks 1-4: complete docketing infrastructure setup and establish
accelerator/VC referral relationships. Weeks 5-9: sign and complete the
first trademark or patent matter. Weeks 10-13: publish first
plain-language IP-strategy content and pursue the first annual-retainer
conversion.

## Scaling Strategy

Build deep expertise in one or two technology verticals first, then
bring on a second patent-bar-qualified associate once docket volume
consistently exceeds solo capacity, then add trademark-portfolio
management as a scalable adjacent service line.

## Exit Opportunities

A documented client portfolio, docketing records, and recurring
IP-retainer history make this sellable to a larger IP boutique or
general-practice firm building out an IP department.

## AI Recommendations

Prioritize AI tools for prior-art search assistance — an
implementation accelerator — while keeping claim drafting and
prosecution strategy fully attorney-led.
